# LifeStance Health — Interview Intel

## Company Context

- **Status:** Public company (NASDAQ: LFST), one of the largest outpatient mental health providers in the US
- **Domain:** Mental health care — therapy, psychiatry, telehealth (70%+ of sessions delivered via telehealth)
- **Scale:** 550+ centers across 33 states; grew rapidly through 53 acquisitions before 2021 IPO
- **Engineering phase:** Active modernization — consolidating legacy EHR systems, building scalable platforms, investing in AI/automation
- **CEO quote (2025):** *"Today, we're incredibly inefficient — we have very little use of technology, whether that's AI or RPA or whatever the case may be."* — signals engineers are walking into a rebuild/transformation, not a mature platform

---

## Interview Process

- **Stage 1:** Recruiter/phone screen — background, role fit, compensation expectations
- **Stage 2:** Hiring Manager interview (+ sometimes additional team members) — behavioral + role-specific technical discussion; described as "an interactive forum to do a deeper dive into your background and the job opportunity"
- **Stage 3:** Offer + verification — reference checks, background check, credentialing
- **Difficulty:** 2.3/5 overall (averaged across all roles, mostly clinical — engineering rounds likely more rigorous)
- **Positive experience rate:** 65% of candidates
- **Time to offer:** ~1 week after final interview (36% of respondents)
- **Take-home/coding screen:** Not publicly confirmed for engineering roles, but not ruled out

---

## Interview Style

- Conversational, behavioral-heavy at the HM stage
- Strong focus on **mission alignment** — mental health access is a cultural screen, not a checkbox
- Behavioral questions confirmed across all roles:
  - "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision without consulting your manager."
  - "Give an example of a time you had to solve a problem that was not straightforward."
  - "Tell me about a time you made a mistake and how you corrected it."
  - "Why do you want to work at LifeStance?" — expects genuine mission connection
- Engineering-specific likely topics (inferred from JDs and company context):
  - Leading a team through a technical transformation or legacy modernization
  - Mentoring junior engineers
  - Balancing technical debt against feature delivery
  - Cross-functional collaboration with non-engineering stakeholders
  - HIPAA/data security compliance in engineering work

## Lead Software Engineer Role — What They're Looking For

- 10+ years software development experience (system/API design, microservices, web apps)
- 7+ years designing data models and high-performance databases
- Formal Tech Lead or Team Lead experience (required)
- Expert TypeScript (Node.js backend + React frontend)
- Large-scale distributed systems design
- Hands-on AWS: Lambda, Fargate, Aurora, Kinesis, ECS
- HIPAA / healthcare data security knowledge
- Mentorship and coaching of engineers
- Cross-functional collaboration: Product, Design, QA, DevOps, Security

## Engineering Culture

**Positive signals:**
- Fully remote engineering roles
- Mission-driven; engineers work alongside clinicians and psychiatrists
- Company explicitly shifting from "throwing bodies at problems" to scalable technology solutions
- Active investment in modernization creates high-impact opportunities

**Concerns:**
- Compensation is below-market vs. FAANG/healthtech peers (~$124k avg for SWE)
- Disorganized internal processes due to legacy of 53 acquisitions
- Overall Glassdoor rating: 3.3/5 (declining slightly)

## Prep Recommendations

1. **Mission alignment is non-negotiable** — prepare a genuine, specific answer for why mental health access matters to you personally or professionally
2. **Legacy modernization stories are gold** — the company is actively transforming acquired systems; experience with technical debt, EHR integration, or regulated healthcare environments is highly valued
3. **TypeScript + Node.js + AWS** — be fluent across the full stack and cloud-native patterns
4. **HIPAA/compliance experience** — expect at least one question on handling regulated data
5. **Lead-level stories** — formal tech lead experience required; prepare STAR stories on mentoring, cross-functional alignment, and technical decision-making
6. **Frame impact in business terms** — this is a mission-driven company; connect technical decisions to patient outcomes and operational efficiency
