0. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Three-scenario, ten-year explicit-period model. Primary underwriting column is
SBC-adjusted Free Cash Flow (= OCF – Capex – SBC), which is the cleanest
measure of per-share value creation after accounting for the real dilution cost
of employee equity compensation.

Adobe is a wide-moat, subscription-dominant software platform with Creative
Cloud (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro), Document Cloud (Acrobat/PDF),
and Adobe Experience Platform (digital marketing/analytics). Firefly generative
AI is now embedded across the creative suite, adding a consumption-credit
monetization layer on top of subscriptions. The stock has fallen ~38% from its
52-week high of $421, creating what appears to be a significant dislocation
versus intrinsic value.

1. MODEL ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

PRIMARY UNDERWRITING COLUMN:   SBC-adjusted Free Cash Flow
                                = OCF – Capex – SBC
                                = "true" equity FCF after dilution cost

WHY SBC-ADJ FCF:
  - Adobe's SBC is ~$1.94B/yr (FY2025), or ~8.2% of revenue — meaningful
  - Reported FCF ($9.85B FY2025) flatters by ignoring SBC dilution cost
  - GAAP EPS obscured by amortization of intangibles from prior acquisitions
  - SBC-adj FCF is the cleanest long-run per-share cash creation metric

DISCOUNT RATES:
  - 9%  (wide moat; dominant market position; subscription model resilience)
  - 11% (AI disruption premium + growth deceleration premium)
  - 13% (stress / combined-risk / bear-case discount rate)

TERMINAL VALUE METHODS (equal-weighted average of both):
  - Perpetuity-growth: 3.0% terminal g in all scenarios
                       TV = FCF_FY36 × (1+g) / (WACC – g)
  - Exit multiple: Bear 18x, Base 22x, Bull 25x SBC-adj FCF FY35

SHARE COUNT:
  Static 404M used throughout. Buyback kicker is real but not modeled explicitly
  (conservative). Note: Q1 FY2026 alone saw 8.1M shares retired at $2,478M
  ($305 avg), suggesting $8–10B/yr buyback capacity — the actual per-share value
  creation vs static-share basis is materially higher.

NET CASH:
  Q1 FY2026: Cash $6.89B – Debt $6.23B = +$0.66B net cash ($1.63/share)
  Treated as static add-on to equity value; not modeled dynamically.

TAX / STRUCTURE:
  Tax embedded in historical OCF (GAAP basis). Adobe's effective GAAP tax rate
  has run 13–16%. Not separately modeled in FCF projections — FCF is post-tax
  by construction (OCF is post-tax).

3. KEY ASSUMPTIONS DISCUSSION
================================================================================

REVENUE GROWTH ASSUMPTIONS:

Bear case rationale (6-4% growth):
  Canva has $3.1B ARR and 230M monthly active users vs Creative Cloud's
  $660/yr subscription. Price delta ($120 Canva Pro vs $660 CC) is increasingly
  difficult to justify for non-professional users. AI-native tools (e.g., Runway,
  Midjourney, Figma AI) are commoditizing individual creative tasks. In this
  scenario, Creative Cloud sees churn acceleration among SMB/hobbyist tiers,
  ARR growth decelerates to 5-6% by FY27-28, and Digital Experience faces
  intensifying headwinds from Salesforce/HubSpot. Revenue CAGR FY25-35: ~4.9%.

Base case rationale (10-6% declining):
  Firefly is currently additive, not disruptive — 22B+ assets generated by
  April 2025, contributing 11% of all new Creative Cloud ARR in FY2024.
  Consumption-based credit monetization adds a new revenue layer on top of
  subscriptions. Creative Cloud's professional moat (Photoshop, Premiere Pro,
  After Effects) remains deep — switching costs for studios and agencies are
  very high. Digital Experience benefits from AI-powered analytics and
  personalization at scale. Adobe tracks to the top of its own FY2026 guidance.
  Revenue CAGR FY25-35: ~8.0%.

Bull case rationale (14-7% growth):
  Firefly becomes a major standalone revenue category; consumptions credits
  ramp to $1B+ by FY28. Digital Experience accelerates as AI agents automate
  content supply chains. Adobe's enterprise relationships (Adobe Experience
  Cloud) land new platform deals. International expansion and emerging markets
  add incremental headroom. Document Cloud (Acrobat AI) grows ahead of
  expectations as PDF AI workflows scale. Revenue CAGR FY25-35: ~10.2%.

FCF MARGIN ASSUMPTIONS:

SBC-adj FCF margins start at FY2025 actuals (33.3%). Expansion thesis:
  - Subscription mix shift (higher-margin); Digital Experience scale
  - Firefly credit revenue is very high-margin (incremental)
  - Capex remains minimal (0.75% of revenue in FY2025)
  - SBC as % of revenue likely stable or slightly declining as revenue grows
  Bear: 32% flat — margin erosion from competitive pricing pressure
  Base: 33.5% → 38% by FY2035 (1.5pp improvement per 2 years, conservative)
  Bull: 34.5% → 40% by FY2032, held — operating leverage from Firefly
