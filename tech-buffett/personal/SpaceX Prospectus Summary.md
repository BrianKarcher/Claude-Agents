## Key Risks & Flags

- **Starship dependency**: The entire growth strategy (V3 satellites, orbital AI compute, Moon/Mars) depends on Starship achieving commercial cadence. Still pre-revenue.
- **Musk control**: 82.4% voting power; "controlled company" exemptions reduce governance protections for public shareholders.
- **AI burn rate**: AI CapEx running at ~$31B annualized pace; operating losses accelerating rapidly.
- **ARPU compression**: Starlink ARPU fell $99 → $66 over three years as the company prioritizes global penetration over price.
- **$29B debt load** with significant interest expense (~$1.9B in 2025, rising).
- **xAI/X integration risk**: The February 2026 merger is very recent; legacy X/Twitter restructuring charges continue.
- **No financial forecasts disclosed** — investors underwrite the thesis on vision, not guidance.

---

## Bottom Line

SpaceX is a three-headed company: a profitable, hyper-scaling satellite ISP (Starlink, printing cash); a launch monopoly investing aggressively in next-gen capacity (Starship R&D burning the Space segment's profits); and an early-stage AI company (xAI/Grok/X, deep in loss mode, absorbing the bulk of CapEx). The $74B IPO is essentially a capital raise to fund the AI buildout, with Starlink's cash flows backstopping the balance sheet. At ~$1.76T implied market cap, investors are paying heavily for the AI optionality and the orbital compute vision — not the current P&L.
