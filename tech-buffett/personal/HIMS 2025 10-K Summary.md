# HIMS & Hers Health — FY2025 10-K Summary
**Annual Report | Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2025**

---

## Business Overview

FY2025 was a year of rapid expansion and heavy reinvestment. Revenue grew 59% to $2.35B, net income was $128M, and Adjusted EBITDA reached $318M (14% margin). However, the year is best understood through three transformative events:

1. **Vertical integration acceleration:** The company acquired a peptide API manufacturing facility (February 2025, rebranded as C S Bio Co.), acquired two international digital health businesses (Zava in Germany/UK/EU in July 2025; Medici in Canada in November 2025), and completed internal conversion of its affiliated pharmacies to wholly-owned subsidiaries. Total CapEx surged to $242.5M (vs. $52.8M in 2024).

2. **Convertible notes issuance:** In May 2025, HIMS issued $1B in 0% convertible senior notes due 2030, raising ~$969M net, fundamentally changing the balance sheet from cash-light to cash-heavy. Proceeds are earmarked for growth investment, including AI compute, pharmacy expansion, and international scaling.

3. **Weight loss compounding peak + impending pivot:** FY2025 represented peak revenues from compounded semaglutide. The regulatory risk that had been building (FDA shortage list removals) materialized, and in March 2026 (just after year-end), the company executed a full strategic pivot to branded GLP-1 products (Wegovy/Zepbound). The seeds of this pivot — including the Novo Nordisk commercial relationship — were laid in H2 2025.

## Key Narrative Points

**Gross margin compression is structural, not transient:** Gross margin has fallen every year (82% → 79% → 74%). The 2025 decline reflects: (1) weight loss products having higher COGS from compounding, packaging, and shorter shipping cadences; (2) growing international revenue which carries lower margin; and (3) lab and new specialty launches which are early-stage. Management guides for further compression before stabilization.

**Marketing leverage is real:** Marketing fell from 51% to 39% of revenue in two years. As the subscriber base matures and organic/referral channels grow, paid customer acquisition becomes less dominant. This is the primary long-term margin expansion lever.

**Capital cycle entering:** The tripling of PP&E and quintupling of CapEx signal a deliberate transition from asset-light to asset-heavy. The peptide facility, owned pharmacies, and lab infrastructure are the cornerstone of the vertical integration strategy. The 2025 FCF hit was the investment cost of this transition.

**International is nascent but real:** FY2025 Rest-of-World Revenue of $134M (+399%) is almost entirely from ZAVA (acquired July 2025) and Medici (acquired November 2025). These are not organic wins, but they establish the international infrastructure for future growth. Eucalyptus (Australia, UK, Germany, Canada, Japan) was announced in February 2026 pending close.

**Balance sheet is now a growth instrument:** The $1B convertible notes give HIMS a war chest ($928M in liquid assets) to fund peptide commercialization, international expansion, and new specialty launches without diluting equity. The 0% coupon means no P&L interest cost.

## Regulatory and Risk Context

**GLP-1 compounding risk materialized:** In the months surrounding the 10-K filing, the FDA removed semaglutide from the drug shortage list (the regulatory backstop for 503A/503B compounding exemptions). This forced the March 2026 pivot away from compounded GLP-1s. The 2025 10-K acknowledges this risk extensively and discusses the strategic shift.

**Peptide facility not yet commercial:** The peptide API manufacturing facility acquired in February 2025 had not commenced commercial operations as of the filing date. Once live, it will be subject to FDA cGMP regulations for API manufacturers, CDPH oversight, and state pharmacy board requirements in each state of distribution.

**Lab testing regulatory complexity:** The November 2025 lab testing launch added CLIA, CMS, and FDA oversight layers. The regulatory environment for laboratory-developed tests (LDTs) remained in flux following the 2025 court ruling vacating FDA's final rule on LDTs.

**Novo Nordisk lawsuit:** Filed February 9, 2026 (post-period); voluntarily dismissed March 9, 2026 without prejudice. Related to semaglutide patent '343. Dismissed coincident with the new commercial partnership being announced.
