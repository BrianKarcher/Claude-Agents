# HIMS & Hers Health — FY2023 10-K Summary
**Annual Report | Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2023**

---

## Business Overview

Hims & Hers is a consumer-first telehealth and digital pharmacy platform. Customers access licensed providers online, receive prescriptions, and have medications shipped directly to their door via the company's owned and partner pharmacies. The platform covers sexual health (ED, premature ejaculation), hair loss, dermatology, mental health, and — beginning Q4 2023 — weight loss via compounded GLP-1 medications. Products are sold under the Hims (men's) and Hers (women's) brands, plus Apostrophe (dermatology). Wholesale non-prescription products are sold through retail partners. 

FY2023 was a year of dramatic scale-up, driven almost entirely by the launch of compounded semaglutide (GLP-1) weight loss offerings in Q4 2023.

## Key Narrative Points

**Weight loss launch (Q4 2023):** The company launched compounded GLP-1 (semaglutide) weight loss offerings in Q4 2023. This was the single most important product event in the company's history. Weight loss quickly became the fastest-growing specialty and would dominate 2024 growth.

**Gross margin improvement (78% → 82%):** The mix shift toward higher-margin subscription offerings (vs. lower-margin one-time purchases) drove margin expansion. GLP-1 products, while initially at lower margins due to manufacturing costs, began contributing positively.

**Rapid subscriber growth:** Subscribers roughly doubled year-over-year, from ~903K to ~1,537K. Monthly ARPU grew as customers shifted from one-time purchases toward longer-cadence subscriptions.

**Path to profitability clear:** The company demonstrated positive OCF ($73.5M) and FCF ($47M) for the first time despite still reporting a GAAP net loss. The Adjusted EBITDA of $49.5M (6% margin) signaled the business model was working.

**Infrastructure investment beginning:** PP&E tripled ($11M → $36M) as HIMS began building internal pharmacy and compounding capacity. This accelerated significantly in subsequent years.

---

## Key Risks Highlighted

- Regulatory risk around compounded GLP-1 medications (FDA compounding exemptions under Sections 503A/503B)
- Heavy reliance on weight loss for incremental growth going forward
- Marketing concentration: paid media spend remained high at 51% of revenue
- Early-stage international ambitions not yet activated
- No proprietary drug formulations — competitive moat dependent on platform/experience, not IP
