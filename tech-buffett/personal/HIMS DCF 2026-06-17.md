## Eucalyptus Adjustment Summary

The original model assumed Eucalyptus would close in H2 2026 and partially credited ~$150M of incremental contribution to 2027E. The actual close on June 2 makes three changes:

1. **2026E revenue raised:** Q1 2026 guidance ($2.8–$3.0B) explicitly excluded Eucalyptus. Adding ~7 months of Eucalyptus contribution at estimated $300M annualized run rate = +$175M to 2026E → revised to **$3,075M**.

3. **Net debt increased:** $240M cash consideration paid at close reduces net cash. Revised net debt: $463M (vs. $223M pre-close).

All downstream years are adjusted upward proportionally. The 2030 revenue target floor of $6.5B is reaffirmed by management; this model now projects modest upside to ~$6,900M given the earlier-than-expected close and incremental Eucalyptus scale.

## Valuation Framework

**Approach:** 10-year explicit UFCF forecast (2026E–2035E) with two terminal value methods:
- **Method 1:** Perpetuity growth (Gordon Growth Model)
- **Method 2:** EV/Adj EBITDA exit multiple

**WACC derivation:**
```
Risk-free rate (10-yr UST):    4.5%
Equity risk premium:           5.5%
Beta (estimated):              1.35   (high-growth health-tech, some volatility)
Cost of equity:               11.9%
Debt: 0% coupon, quasi-equity; weight negligible
WACC (rounded):               12.0%
```

**Notes on WACC choice:** HIMS is a profitable, high-growth company with no recurring interest expense and a dominant equity capital structure. A 12% discount rate reflects execution risk (GLP-1 pivot, margin compression), a relatively short track record of sustained profitability, and international integration uncertainty from four acquisitions in 18 months. The Eucalyptus close reduces some uncertainty (the deal was a known risk) but adds integration complexity.

## Terminal Value

### Method 1: Perpetuity Growth (Gordon Growth Model)

```
Terminal FCF (2036E)  = 2035E UFCF × (1 + g)
                      = $1,465M × 1.035 = $1,516M

Terminal Value (2035) = $1,516M / (WACC – g)
                      = $1,516M / (12.0% – 3.5%)
                      = $1,516M / 8.5%
                      = $17,835M

PV of Terminal Value  = $17,835M × 0.3296
                      = $5,879M

Implied exit Adj EBITDA multiple (check): $17,835M / $2,825M = 6.3x  ← conservative
```

**Terminal growth rate rationale (3.5%):** HIMS has built a global consumer health platform with subscription flywheel economics, proprietary closed-loop clinical data, and network effects that compound with scale. A 3.5% terminal growth rate — in line with nominal GDP — is conservative given the addressable market and AI-driven personalization optionality. Peptides, future specialty expansion, and orbital/emerging health categories could push growth structurally above this.

### Method 2: EV/Adj EBITDA Exit Multiple

```
2035E Adj EBITDA = $2,825M

Exit Multiple:
  Bear:   10x  →  TV = $28,250M  →  PV = $9,309M
  Base:   13x  →  TV = $36,725M  →  PV = $12,105M
  Bull:   16x  →  TV = $45,200M  →  PV = $14,899M
```

**Exit multiple rationale:** At 13x 2035E Adj EBITDA, HIMS would carry a premium consistent with a durable, scaled global subscription health platform growing at 7%/yr with 25% EBITDA margins. This is not aggressive relative to comparable-quality platforms — it is essentially what you'd pay for a scaled subscription business with strong retention and global optionality.

## Key Value Drivers & Watch Items

**What makes this work (upside):**
1. **Subscriber retention compounds:** Combined platform now serves 3.4M+ subscribers. If multi-year retention holds, LTV per subscriber is enormous and the denominator for CAC keeps expanding.
2. **Peptide category legitimization (July 2026):** FDA Category 1 classification would unlock a large TAM with HIMS the best-positioned compliant provider (domestic API facility, 503A compounding scale, clinical protocols). Meaningful upside not fully modeled in base case.
3. **Marketing leverage continues:** Trend from 51% → 39% of revenue in three years. Every additional point of leverage on $4B+ revenue = $40M+ in EBITDA. Brand awareness is compounding.
4. **International flywheel now real:** Eucalyptus closed and operational. AU/UK/DE/JP/CA markets combined give HIMS a global data set and scale. Incremental subscribers in these markets contribute at high marginal EBITDA once platform costs are amortized.
5. **AI platform moat deepening:** Proprietary closed-loop clinical data from millions of patients across multiple geographies. Clinician-verified training signals. Hard to replicate; getting harder each quarter.

**What breaks the thesis (downside):**
1. **Branded GLP-1 economics disappoint:** If Novo/Lilly tighten distribution terms, compress pricing, or pull the partnership, the weight loss specialty margin structure collapses and takes subscriber growth with it.
2. **Eucalyptus integration harder than expected:** Four major international acquisitions in 18 months (Zava, Medici, Livewell, Eucalyptus). Regulatory complexity across 8+ jurisdictions, differing healthcare systems, and cultural product-market fit is significant.
3. **Peptide regulation stays murky or unfavorable:** Any adverse FDA ruling extends the investment period at the peptide facility with no revenue offset.
4. **Marketing efficiency reverses:** Significant dependency on digital advertising. Platform algorithm changes or rising CPMs could spike CAC materially.
5. **Convertible note overhang:** If the stock rises above $70.69, the $1B convert creates 14.1M+ dilutive shares (partially hedged by capped calls up to the cap price). Manageable but worth tracking at higher valuations.

## Preliminary Rating

**WATCH / ACCUMULATE ON CONFIRMATION**

At the base case intrinsic value of ~$35–60/share (average ~$48), HIMS is **reasonably to moderately undervalued** depending on the current market price. The Eucalyptus close is a meaningful de-risking event — the largest pending deal is now done, adding 850K customers and 8 markets immediately.

**Remaining confirmation needed before conviction buy:**
1. Q2 2026 results confirming branded GLP-1 subscriber momentum (100K+/month on track)
2. FDA peptide Category 1 classification for key molecules (July 2026 — potential catalyst)
3. Q2/Q3 EBITDA margin trajectory toward 15–16% in H2 2026 (guided back-half loading)

**The market is discounting peptides, AI platform monetization, and most of the international optionality as zero or near-zero.** Eucalyptus closing on schedule removes the deal-risk discount. If peptides are green-lit in July 2026, intrinsic value moves firmly toward the bull case ($80–100). If execution on GLP-1 pivot disappoints, the $22–27 bear case is the floor.
