# Hims & Hers Health (HIMS) — Q1 2026 Summary
**Q1 Earnings Call (May 11, 2026) + 10-Q (Period ended March 31, 2026)**

---

## The Big Story: Weight Loss Strategic Pivot

The quarter was defined by one decision made in March 2026: HIMS discontinued advertising of compounded GLP-1 products and shifted entirely to branded weight loss medications (Wegovy, Zepbound). Management described it as creating "near-term financial noise to unlock immense potential." The noise was real — US revenue fell 8% YoY — but the early signals from the pivot have been strong enough to raise full-year guidance.

## Weight Loss Pivot: Details

**What changed:** In March 2026, HIMS stopped advertising compounded semaglutide and pivoted to act as a distribution platform for branded GLP-1s (Wegovy from Novo Nordisk; Zepbound access through LillyDirect). HIMS recognizes revenue on a **gross basis** for branded drugs filled through its own pharmacies.

**Early results:**
- 125,000+ Wegovy shipments fulfilled within 6 weeks of launch
- On track for 100,000+ new weight loss subscribers per month
- ~90% of new branded weight loss users downloaded the app
- Average new subscriber interacted with a provider 3x in their first month
- New weight loss demand exceeding even the New Year's / Super Bowl campaign peaks from prior years

**Unit economics:** Dollar contribution per subscriber is roughly equivalent between branded and legacy compounded — the pivot is about expanding the addressable market, not boosting per-unit economics.

**Note on Novo Nordisk lawsuit:** Novo Nordisk filed a patent infringement suit against HIMS in February 2026 (U.S. Patent No. 8,12,343 on semaglutide compositions, expiring December 2031). Novo voluntarily dismissed without prejudice on March 9, 2026 — shortly after announcing the commercial partnership. The case could theoretically be refiled.

## Peptide Opportunity

This was the hottest topic on the call — more than half of retail investor questions submitted were about peptides.

### What HIMS is building

HIMS acquired a **dedicated peptide manufacturing facility** (and related assets) in **February 2025**. The facility is not yet in commercial operation. The investment thesis is vertical integration: by manufacturing peptide APIs (active pharmaceutical ingredients) domestically, HIMS controls quality, purity, and supply chain reliability end-to-end — an advantage they argue will be impossible for competitors without domestic infrastructure to replicate.

Their 503A compounding footprint (~1 million+ sq ft of pharmacy infrastructure) enables personalized dosing and formulations at scale. The combination of in-house API manufacturing and 503A compounding capability means HIMS can customize dose, concentration, and formulation per patient — distinct from simply dispensing off-the-shelf branded products.

HIMS is also investing in clinical experts and protocols, and plans to integrate peptide therapies with their lab testing capabilities so patients can track biomarkers and providers can monitor outcomes over time.

### FDA Regulatory Timeline

The FDA is in the process of **classifying peptides** under its bulk drug substances program. Andrew Dudum cited **July 2026** as a key regulatory milestone when the FDA is expected to provide clarity on which peptides may be designated **"Category 1"** — i.e., eligible for use in 503A compounding because the FDA has found clinical need. Additional guidance is expected by **year-end 2026**.

This matters because the current peptide market is described by HIMS management as a **"gray market"**: products exist but are unregulated, inconsistent in quality, and typically lack clinical support. A Category 1 designation would legitimize the category for regulated compounders like HIMS.

HIMS has explicitly stated they will **not be first to market** but intend to be **best in market** — they will not launch until their standards are fully met.

### Regulatory Requirements (from 10-Q)

Once commercial operations begin at the peptide facility, HIMS will be subject to:
- **FDA cGMP regulations** for registered API manufacturers
- **CDPH Food & Drug Branch** oversight as a California-registered drug manufacturer
- **State pharmacy board regulations** for every state to which they ship peptide APIs
- **503A compounding rules** (FDCA Sections 503A/503B) for any compounded end products
- Potential additional state licensure requirements as the API is introduced into new markets

The 10-Q explicitly flags peptide development as a new, material regulatory risk area alongside telehealth, compounding, and lab testing.

### Medications / Categories of Interest

HIMS did not name specific peptides in these filings. However, based on the regulatory and clinical context:
- Management references **longevity and proactive health** as the primary consumer demand driver — consistent with common peptides such as BPC-157 (tissue repair/gut health), CJC-1295/Ipamorelin (growth hormone secretagogue stack), PT-141/Bremelanotide (sexual health), TB-500/Thymosin Beta-4, and AOD-9604 (metabolic)
- The GLP-1 experience is explicitly framed as a gateway: "patients looking to be more proactive with health... preventative and longevity focused"
- Both **men's and women's** applications are mentioned — demand described as "extremely large" on both sides
- Peptides are grouped with testosterone, menopause, and labs as the new wave of proactive health specialties

### Competitive Position

HIMS argues their advantage is four-layered:
1. **Domestic peptide API manufacturing** (facility acquired Feb 2025) — controlling purity, quality, supply chain
2. **503A compounding footprint** (~1M+ sq ft) for personalized dosing and formulations
3. **Clinical protocols and physician oversight** — physician-first approach vs. unregulated gray-market sellers
4. **Lab testing integration** — ability to monitor patient biomarkers and outcomes over time

**No peptide revenue has been booked yet.** The opportunity is entirely pre-revenue. Management is deliberately waiting for the July 2026 FDA classification guidance before launching.

## Key Risks

- **GLP-1 pivot execution:** Branded product margins may compress further; monthly-cadence customers have inherently higher acquisition and fulfillment costs than long-cycle prepaid customers.
- **Novo Nordisk lawsuit:** Dismissed without prejudice — could be refiled, particularly if the commercial relationship sours.
- **Gross margin compression:** Guidance implies several more points of decline in Q2 before recovery in H2.
- **Peptide regulatory risk:** Commercial operations haven't started at the peptide facility. Full cGMP compliance, CDPH registration, and state pharmacy board approvals required before revenue can be generated. FDA Category 1 designations in July 2026 are not guaranteed for all targeted peptides.
- **International integration complexity:** ZAVA, Livewell/Medici, and pending Eucalyptus represent significant operational and regulatory complexity across multiple jurisdictions (UK, EU, Canada, Australia, Japan).
- **Debt load:** $974M in 2030 convertible notes — no current cash interest burden but substantial dilution potential on conversion.
- **GAAP profitability:** Net loss of $92M in Q1 2026; return to net income not expected until 2027.
