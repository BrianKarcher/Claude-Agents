SECTION 1 — BUSINESS SNAPSHOT
================================================================================

AMD is a fabless semiconductor company (TSMC-manufactured) competing across
four segments. FY2025 revenue mix:

  - Data Center (EPYC server CPUs + Instinct AI GPUs):  $16,635M  48.0%  +32.2% YoY
  - Client (Ryzen desktop/mobile CPUs):                 $10,640M  30.7%  +50.9% YoY
  - Gaming (Radeon GPUs + semi-custom console silicon):  $3,910M   11.3%  +50.7% YoY
  - Embedded (FPGA/adaptive SoC, ex-Xilinx):              $3,454M   10.0%   -2.9% YoY
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Total FY2025 revenue:                                 $34,639M            +34.3% YoY

By Q1 2026, Data Center had grown to 56.3% of total revenue ($5,775M of
$10,253M), up from 49.4% a year earlier — the mix shift toward AI compute is
happening fast and is the entire investment thesis.

The thesis in three sentences:
  1. AMD has gone from "credible #2 to Nvidia in AI accelerators, credible #1
     alternative to Intel in server CPUs" to a company management now says is
     on track for "tens of billions of dollars in annual data center AI
     revenue in 2027" — the Instinct MI450/Helios rack-scale platform ramps
     production in H2 2026, backed by multi-gigawatt commitments from Meta
     and OpenAI.
  2. Server CPU demand is inflecting independently of the AI GPU story:
     management raised its server CPU TAM estimate from ~$60B (Oct 2025
     Analyst Day) to >$120B by 2030 in a single quarter, driven by agentic
     AI's CPU-orchestration requirements, and guided Q2 2026 server CPU
     revenue growth of >70% YoY.
  3. The business is inflecting operationally: Q1 2026 revenue +38% YoY,
     GAAP operating margin expanded from 7.4% (FY24) to 14.4% (Q1 26), and
     free cash flow more than tripled YoY to a record $2.6B in the quarter
     (25% of revenue) — this is not a story requiring imagination, the
     numbers are already moving.

Key pending items (as of July 2026):
  - Meta partnership (Feb 2026): up to 6 gigawatts of AMD Instinct GPUs over
    multiple product generations, first gigawatt on custom MI450-based
    silicon. AMD issued Meta a warrant for up to 160M shares (~9.7% of
    shares outstanding) at a $0.01 strike, vesting on GPU purchase milestones
    and AMD stock price targets. None vested as of Q1 2026 — this is a real,
    uncapped-until-triggered dilution overhang not reflected in my share
    count (see Section 8).
  - OpenAI partnership (previously announced, referenced again this quarter)
    — another large multi-generation Instinct commitment.
  - MI450/Helios: sampling to lead customers now; production ramp begins
    H2 2026 (initial volume Q3, "significant ramp" Q4). This product carries
    below-corporate-average gross margin at launch — a near-term margin
    headwind even as it's the long-term growth driver.
  - AMD's "Advancing AI" event in July 2026 (this month) is expected to
    detail next-gen Instinct, EPYC (Venice), and Helios roadmap — could move
    estimates materially in either direction.
  - Long-term target reiterated on the call: management sees "a clear path
    to exceed" prior long-term targets, "including delivering more than $20
    in EPS over the strategic time frame" (non-GAAP, multi-year horizon,
    unconfirmed exact date — treated with skepticism in this model; see
    Section 4).

SECTION 3 — STRATEGIC CONTEXT: THE AI RAMP AND THE DILUTION OVERHANG
================================================================================

Management commentary this quarter (Lisa Su, Jean Hu) contained an unusually
large amount of forward-leaning color, most of it bullish. Key data points
extracted directly from the transcript:

  - Server CPU TAM raised from ~$60B (Oct 2025 Analyst Day, ~18% CAGR) to
    >$120B by 2030 (>35% CAGR) — a doubling of the addressable market
    estimate in roughly one quarter, attributed to agentic AI's CPU
    orchestration requirements (head nodes, data movement, parallel
    execution for AI agents).
  - Data center AI: "strong and increasing confidence in our ability to
    deliver tens of billions of dollars in annual data center AI revenue in
    2027, and to exceed our long-term growth target of greater than 80% [CAGR]
    in the coming years."
  - Q2 2026 guidance: revenue ~$11.2B (+46% YoY), non-GAAP gross margin ~56%,
    server CPU revenue growth >70% YoY continuing through H2 2026.
  - Gross margin long-term target reiterated at 55-58% (from the Oct 2025
    Analyst Day), with management stating Q1 2026 is "making good progress"
    toward it.
  - "$20 in EPS over the strategic time frame" — this is a non-GAAP,
    multi-year aspirational target restated from the Analyst Day, not a
    dated commitment. At ~1.65B diluted shares, $20 non-GAAP EPS implies
    roughly $33B of non-GAAP net income — a bar this model's Bull case does
    not clear even by 2036 on a comparable cash basis (Bull case 2036 NOPAT
    is $59.8B pre-add-backs, but non-GAAP EPS and after-tax owner earnings
    are not the same metric; treat the $20 figure as promotional framing,
    not a modeling anchor).

THE META WARRANT — a real, quantifiable dilution risk not in my base share
count: as part of the February 2026 agreement for Meta to deploy up to 6
gigawatts of AMD Instinct GPUs, AMD issued Meta a warrant for up to 160
million shares at a $0.01 exercise price (~9.7% of the current 1,650M
diluted share count). Vesting is tied to (a) Meta's GPU purchase milestones
and (b) AMD stock price targets. None of the tranches had vested as of
Q1 2026. If materially vested, this is a mechanical ~9-10% reduction to
per-share intrinsic value, roughly offsetting a full year of assumed
Base-case FCF growth. I have NOT built this into the base share count
because vesting is contingent and the trigger prices/volumes are not
disclosed in the filings provided — but any purchase decision should treat
per-share IV as ~9% lower than shown here on a fully-diluted basis if the
warrant looks likely to vest.

MI450 MARGIN DRAG: management was explicit that MI450/Helios "will start to
ramp in Q3 and ramp significantly in Q4 [2026]. That is below corporate
average [gross margin]." This is a real near-term margin headwind that the
model's FY26E operating margin (17.5%) and 2027E margin (Base: 19%) already
attempt to reflect conservatively rather than extrapolating Q1's 14.4% GAAP
margin straight up.

MODELING APPROACH
- 10-year explicit forecast: 2027E-2036E, off a FY2026E anchor built
  bottom-up from Q1 actual + Q2 guidance + qualitative H2 color (below)
- Unlevered FCF (FCFF) approach: FCF = NOPAT + D&A (incl. acquisition
  amortization) - CapEx; a second metric, "SBC-adjusted FCF," further
  subtracts stock-based compensation as a real economic (if non-cash) cost
  to shareholders — SBC-adjusted FCF is what is discounted and used for
  terminal value
- NOPAT = GAAP operating income x (1 - 15% effective tax rate)
- Net cash (not net debt) added back to enterprise value — AMD carries only
  $3.2B of debt against $12.3B of cash/investments
- Each scenario (Bull/Base/Bear) uses its OWN discount rate and terminal
  growth assumption, not just different operating assumptions — a scenario
  where AMD executes flawlessly for a decade also, in practice, carries less
  residual risk than one where competition and execution disappoint. This
  mirrors the CHTR/HIMS DCFs in this folder.

SECTION 7 — SCENARIO ANALYSIS
================================================================================

BULL CASE (~$357/share; 10.0% WACC, 3.5% TGR, 26x exit)
  - Data center AI revenue reaches the "tens of billions" range in 2027 as
    guided and continues compounding near an 80%+ CAGR for several more years
  - Server CPU captures >50% share of a $120B+ TAM by 2030 (management's
    stated target)
  - Helios ramps in H2 2026 without material supply, power, or yield issues
  - Gross margin reaches the top of the 55-58% long-term target range and
    GAAP operating margin scales to 30% as amortization runs off and mix
    shifts further toward Data Center
  - No major competitive share loss to Nvidia's next-gen platforms or
    hyperscaler custom silicon (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, etc.)
  - Meta and OpenAI relationships expand rather than plateau; new large
    AI-native customers materialize (as management hinted on the call)

BASE CASE (~$167/share; 11.5% WACC, 3.0% TGR, 20x exit)
  - Data center AI and server CPU both grow very fast but below management's
    most bullish framing; execution on the multi-generation Helios ramp is
    real but bumpier than guided
  - Client/gaming soften in H2 2026 as guided (memory cost inflation,
    gaming down >20% H2 vs H1) and grow modestly thereafter
  - Gross margin reaches the low end of the 55-58% target range; GAAP
    operating margin plateaus at 25%
  - Some competitive share pressure from Nvidia's roadmap and growing
    hyperscaler custom-silicon programs caps upside without derailing growth

BEAR CASE (~$57/share; 13.5% WACC, 2.0% TGR, 14x exit)
  - Nvidia's incumbency (CUDA ecosystem, next-gen Rubin platform) and/or
    hyperscaler custom ASICs (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia,
    OpenAI's own silicon ambitions) cap AMD's AI accelerator share gains
    well below the "tens of billions in 2027" framing
  - Server CPU TAM growth disappoints as agentic AI CPU-demand claims prove
    overstated, or Intel's supply/competitive position improves faster than
    expected
  - Memory cost inflation (DRAM/HBM tightness) compresses client and gaming
    demand more than the guided >20% H2 gaming decline
  - China export restrictions tighten further, permanently removing a
    meaningful revenue pool (already saw a sequential AI revenue dip in Q1
    2026 attributed to "the China transition")
  - Helios ramp encounters yield, power, or supply-chain delays, pushing the
    AI margin inflection out 1-2 years
  - Meta warrant vests at scale, adding realized dilution on top of
    disappointing fundamentals (a genuine "worst of both worlds" tail risk)

SECTION 8 — KEY RISKS
================================================================================

1. VALUATION / MULTIPLE COMPRESSION (VERY HIGH — the dominant risk today)
   At $517/share (~$844B market cap), AMD trades at ~17x FY2026E revenue and,
   per this model, at 3.1x base-case intrinsic value and 1.45x even the Bull
   case. News flow around the current price already shows the fragility of
   this: the stock fell 6.5% in a single session this week on competitive
   fears (a Chinese AI developer reportedly designing its own processor) even
   as Goldman Sachs simultaneously raised its price target to $640. A stock
   priced for perfection reacts violently to any data point that isn't
   perfect. This is the single largest risk to an investor buying today,
   independent of whether the underlying business execution is good.

2. CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION & THE META WARRANT (HIGH)
   The AI growth story now rests heavily on a small number of hyperscaler
   relationships (Meta, OpenAI). The Meta warrant (up to 160M shares, ~9.7%
   of shares outstanding, $0.01 strike) is a direct, quantifiable dilution
   risk tied to Meta's own purchasing behavior and AMD's stock price — a
   structure that gives Meta significant negotiating leverage on future
   pricing while AMD shareholders absorb the dilution. See Section 3.

3. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING VS. NVIDIA (HIGH)
   Nvidia remains far larger, better-capitalized, and has a much stronger
   software moat (CUDA) in AI accelerators. AMD's ROCm software stack is
   improving (management cited MLPerf results and expanded model support)
   but is not at parity. AMD's AI GPU share gains are real but could stall
   if Nvidia's roadmap (or aggressive pricing) outpaces expectations.

4. HYPERSCALER CUSTOM SILICON (MEDIUM-HIGH)
   Google (TPU), Amazon (Trainium), Microsoft (Maia), and reportedly OpenAI
   itself are all developing custom AI accelerators. To the extent AMD's
   largest potential customers are simultaneously its long-term competitors
   in silicon, the TAM AMD can actually capture may be structurally smaller
   than the "TAM" figures management cites.

5. GROSS MARGIN DILUTION FROM THE MI450 RAMP (MEDIUM, near-term)
   Management explicitly flagged that MI450/Helios will carry below-
   corporate-average gross margin during the H2 2026 ramp. This is already
   reflected in the model's conservative FY2026E/2027E margin assumptions
   but is a real quarter-to-quarter risk to consensus estimates through 2027.

6. MEMORY COST INFLATION (MEDIUM)
   Rising DRAM/HBM prices are a genuine industry-wide dynamic (also affecting
   Nvidia, Google, everyone building AI infrastructure). Management flagged
   H2 2026 demand risk in client and gaming specifically from this. A
   sustained memory shortage could also constrain the pace of AI accelerator
   deployment industry-wide, independent of AMD's own execution.

7. CYCLICALITY (MEDIUM)
   Client and gaming (41% of FY2025 revenue) remain cyclical, PC/console-
   demand-driven businesses. A broader consumer or enterprise IT spending
   slowdown would hit these segments before the AI-driven Data Center
   segment, which is running on multi-year committed capacity deals.

8. CHINA / EXPORT CONTROLS (MEDIUM)
   Q1 2026 data center AI revenue actually declined sequentially, which
   management attributed to "the China transition" — export control policy
   is a real, non-hypothetical revenue swing factor, evidenced by the ~$440M
   of net inventory/related charges tied to MI308 export controls disclosed
   in the FY2025 10-K.

9. EXECUTION RISK ON A GENUINELY HARD PRODUCT (MEDIUM)
   Helios is a rack-scale, multi-generation, co-engineered platform (GPUs +
   EPYC CPUs + networking, integrated with named hyperscaler deployments
   down to "which data centers the GPUs going into"). This is a much more
   complex execution challenge than a standalone chip launch, with more
   points of failure.

SECTION 9 — INVESTMENT ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

POSITIVES (WHY IT COULD STILL WORK FROM HERE)
  + The underlying business is genuinely inflecting, not just guided to
    inflect: Q1 2026 revenue +38% YoY, FCF more than tripled YoY to a
    record $2.6B (25% of revenue), operating margin expanding sequentially
  + Server CPU TAM raise (~$60B to >$120B by 2030) is a real, independently
    verifiable data point, not just AI-accelerator hype — and it comes with
    a >70% YoY Q2 guide already booked
  + Multi-gigawatt, multi-generation commitments from Meta and OpenAI provide
    genuine multi-year revenue visibility ("down to which data centers the
    GPUs are going to be installed in," per Lisa Su)
  + Fabless model means capex intensity is a fraction of hyperscaler AI
    capex — FCF conversion on incremental revenue is structurally high
  + Net cash balance sheet ($9.1B net cash) — zero balance-sheet risk,
    unlike a levered story like CHTR in this same folder
  + If the bull case plays out, $357/share is still meaningfully below
    today's $517 print, which sets a real bar: the market needs the AI
    ramp to outperform even the most optimistic scenario I can construct
    from management's own words

NEGATIVES (WHY THIS IS NOT A BUY AT $517)
  - Every scenario in this model — including Bull — produces an intrinsic
    value below the current price. Closing the gap requires assumptions I
    am not willing to make: either a ~7.5% discount rate on Bull-case cash
    flows (implying AMD is nearly as safe as an investment-grade bond
    issuer), or 2036 revenue near $340B (larger than any semiconductor
    company today, in a market AMD would have to largely create or take
    from Nvidia, Intel, and every custom-silicon program simultaneously)
  - The Meta warrant is real, disclosed, uncapped-until-triggered dilution
    that is not in the current share count or in most sell-side models
  - The stock's own recent behavior (-6.5% in a single session this week on
    competitive headlines, despite a same-week price target increase from
    Goldman) confirms the market has priced in very little room for error
  - This is a much higher-variance bet than the portfolio's current holdings
    (MSFT, MNDY): AMD's fate over the next 3 years is genuinely more
    uncertain, tied to execution on a hard multi-generation product ramp
    against a far larger competitor

BOTTOM LINE
AMD is a real business inflecting on real demand, and the qualitative case
for continued share gains in AI accelerators and server CPUs is stronger
than it has been in years. But price and value are different things. At
$517/share the market is not pricing "AMD executes well" — it is pricing
"AMD executes at the very top of what management itself has guided to, for
a decade, at a discount rate more appropriate for a monopoly infrastructure
asset than a semiconductor challenger." That is not a bet this model can
underwrite. If AMD pulled back toward $150-200/share, this becomes a
genuinely interesting, high-conviction name; at $517 it is a pass.

Conviction level on base case: MODERATE (business quality) / LOW (as a
purchase at current price)
Would need to see: a pullback of 60%+ from current levels, OR a materially
de-risked view of the Helios ramp (2-3 quarters of clean execution data)
that would justify moving the Bull case's discount rate meaningfully below
10% — neither is likely to happen quickly.
