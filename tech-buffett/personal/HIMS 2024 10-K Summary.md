# HIMS & Hers Health — FY2024 10-K Summary
**Annual Report | Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2024**

---

## Business Overview

FY2024 was HIMS's breakout year. The company achieved GAAP net income profitability for the first time, driven almost entirely by explosive growth in compounded GLP-1 (semaglutide) weight loss offerings. Revenue grew 69% to $1.48B, and the company generated $126M in net income and $176.9M in Adjusted EBITDA. The GLP-1 opportunity proved real and massive — but also created a near-total revenue dependency on compounded semaglutide that would become a strategic vulnerability in 2026. The company also began a meaningful share repurchase program, deployed capital into fulfillment infrastructure, and acquired MedisourceRx to vertically integrate its pharmacy supply chain.

## Key Narrative Points

**First full-year GAAP profitability:** FY2024 was the company's first ever profitable year on a net income basis ($126M). Even excluding the one-time $54M tax benefit, the company was meaningfully GAAP-positive for the first time.

**GLP-1 concentration risk building:** By FY2024, weight loss (primarily compounded semaglutide) had become the dominant growth driver. This concentration in a single category with regulatory risk was becoming material.

**Gross margin compression:** The shift toward weight loss products (which have higher COGS from manufacturing and shorter shipping cadences) pushed gross margin from 82% to 79%. Management guided for further compression as the weight loss mix grew.

**Marketing leverage emerging:** Marketing fell from 51% to 46% of revenue as the Subscriber base matured and organic/referral channels grew. This was an important efficiency signal.

**Infrastructure scaling:** PP&E grew 67% ($36M → $60M) but remained modest relative to revenue. The major capital investment cycle had not yet begun — that would come in 2025 with the peptide facility acquisition and pharmacy expansion.

**Tax benefit inflated reported net income:** The $54.3M partial valuation allowance release boosted GAAP net income well above run-rate. Investors should treat normalized net income of ~$72M as the cleaner measure.

---

## Key Risks Highlighted

- **Regulatory risk on compounded GLP-1s:** Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly were pushing the FDA to remove semaglutide/tirzepatide from the shortage list, which would end the 503A/503B compounding exemptions and make compounded GLP-1s illegal. Management monitored this closely.
- **Gross margin trajectory:** Continued mix shift toward lower-margin weight loss products was eroding the exceptional margins of the core sexual health/hair loss specialties.
- **Customer acquisition cost dependency:** While improving, marketing was still 46% of revenue — a meaningful sensitivity to digital advertising costs and platform algorithm changes.
- **Single-category concentration:** Despite stated ambitions for a multi-specialty platform, weight loss was becoming so dominant that it created meaningful thesis risk if the regulatory environment changed.
