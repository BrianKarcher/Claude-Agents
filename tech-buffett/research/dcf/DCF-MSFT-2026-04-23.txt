B. KEY ASSUMPTIONS
================================================================================

B.1 Discount Rate: 10.0%
-------------------------
MSFT warrants a lower discount rate than the 12-14% used for smaller,
riskier names because:
  - Investment-grade balance sheet (AA+ rated, $49B net cash)
  - Diversified revenue across cloud, productivity, gaming, search
  - Recurring/contractual revenue >70% of total
  - 45%+ operating margins with expanding trajectory
  - Near-zero probability of permanent capital loss

10% represents a reasonable equity hurdle for a mega-cap quality compounder.

B.2 Shares Outstanding: 7,430M (flat across forecast)
------------------------------------------------------
Buybacks (~$18-20B/yr) roughly offset SBC ($12-13B/yr) plus organic
dilution. Net share count declining ~5-8M/yr -- immaterial at this scale.
Conservatively assume flat at 7,430M.

B.3 Revenue Growth Assumptions (by scenario)
---------------------------------------------
Starting point: FY2026E revenue of $323B (H1 actual $158.9B + guided
H2 of ~$164B based on Q3 guide midpoint of $81.2B + estimated Q4 ~$83B).

Rationale:
  - Bull: Azure sustains 30%+ through FY28, Copilot reaches 15%+ M365
    penetration, AI revenue scales to $50B+ by FY28. Growth decelerates
    gradually from the law of large numbers.
  - Base: Azure decelerates to 25% by FY28, Copilot reaches 8-12%
    penetration, overall growth moderates to low-teens by FY29. This is
    the "everything works but nothing is heroic" case.
  - Bear: AI capex produces subpar returns, OpenAI defects, Copilot
    stalls below 5%, macro headwinds. Growth drops to single digits by
    FY29 as the company matures into a utility-like grower.

    15 years.

B.5 Capex Assumptions ($ billions)
------------------------------------
This is the most critical assumption set. H1 FY2026 capex was $49.3B,
annualizing to ~$99B. MSFT CFO guided FY2026 capex growth rate "higher
than FY2025" ($64.6B), implying $95-105B for FY2026.

FY2026 capex estimate: $100B for all scenarios (actual H1 run rate).

Rationale:
  - Bull: Capex peaks in FY2026-27 and declines as supply catches demand.
    By FY2031 MSFT reaches maintenance-level capex of ~$55B (~7% of rev).
  - Base: Elevated capex persists through FY2028 as MSFT continues the
    datacenter buildout. Moderates to $60B by FY2033 (~6-7% of rev).
  - Bear: Capex stays stubbornly high because demand disappoints and MSFT
    has committed to long-term supply contracts. Stranded asset risk is
    real. Capex to revenue ratio stays elevated at 8-10%.

B.6 Tax Rate: 18% (effective, aligned with recent actuals)
B.7 SBC as % of Revenue: 4.0% (stable, per H1 FY2026 trend of 3.9%)
B.8 D&A Growth: Modeled implicitly via operating margin assumptions
    (D&A is the primary margin headwind in the near term)

B.9 Terminal Value Assumptions
-------------------------------
Two methods, averaged:

Method 1 -- Perpetuity Growth:
  - Terminal FCF growth rate: 3.0% (all scenarios)
  - Terminal value = FCF_yr10 * (1 + g) / (WACC - g) = FCF * 1.03 / 0.07

Method 2 -- Exit Multiple:
  - Bull: 25x SBC-adjusted FCF (premium quality compounder)
  - Base: 22x SBC-adjusted FCF (in line with historical avg)
  - Bear: 18x SBC-adjusted FCF (de-rated, utility-like)

B.10 Probability Weights
--------------------------
  - Bull: 25%
  - Base: 50%
  - Bear: 25%

I. WHAT THE MARKET IS PRICING
================================================================================

At $416.51, the market is pricing approximately:
  - 10% discount rate: slightly below our base case ($454), implying
    the market sees base-case execution as ~85% likely with some bear-
    case tail risk. This seems reasonable.
  - 12% discount rate: the stock would need bull-case execution to
    justify current price. At 12%, our probability-weighted IV is only
    $336, meaning the stock is overvalued unless you accept a lower
    hurdle.
  - Forward P/E: ~25x FY2026E adj EPS of ~$16.50 (consensus). This is
    at the low end of MSFT's 5-year range (28-33x) but not cheap in
    absolute terms.

Implied assumptions at $416:
  - Revenue CAGR of ~12-13% over 10 years (between base and bull)
  - Operating margins stabilizing at 47-48%
  - Capex declining from current $100B/yr to $60-65B by FY2032
  - Terminal multiple of ~22x SBC-adjusted FCF

These are reasonable but not conservative assumptions.
