B. ASSUMPTIONS — THREE SCENARIOS
================================================================================

DISCOUNT RATE (WACC)
- Risk-free rate: 4.3% (10-yr UST, April 2026)
- Equity risk premium: 5.5%
- Beta: 1.8 (5-yr weekly, elevated due to recent volatility)
- Cost of equity: 4.3% + 1.8 * 5.5% = 14.2%
- Debt: $0 (no traditional debt; ignore lease obligations for WACC)
- WACC = Cost of equity = 14.2%
- HURDLE RATE: We use 12% as specified (more generous than WACC; if the
  model clears 12%, it more than clears our conviction threshold; if it
  clears 14.2%, it clears a pure-CAPM hurdle too).

We present all NPV calculations at BOTH 12% and 14% discount rates.

SHARE COUNT ASSUMPTIONS
- 2025 actual: 494M diluted
- We model -2% per year net (buybacks at depressed prices minus SBC grants)
- By 2035: ~405M diluted shares

VENTURA ASSUMPTION (all scenarios)
- Ventura OS: $0 revenue contribution across all 10 years
- Ventura R&D cost: embedded in the opex structure but generates nothing
- This is a deliberate zero-credit approach per the deep-dive mandate

SBC TREATMENT
- SBC modeled as growing 3% per year from $491M base (reflects RSU refresh
  grants and new hires, partially offset by lower grant prices)
- SBC is subtracted from reported FCF to produce SBC-adjusted FCF
- All intrinsic values are derived from SBC-adjusted FCF

KEY MARGIN BUILD (BASE CASE)

- This implies TTD merely HOLDS its current share of a growing TAM
- Entirely plausible even with Amazon share gains in CTV
