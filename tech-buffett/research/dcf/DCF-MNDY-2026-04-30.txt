  1.2  DCF METHODOLOGY (per deep dive sec 10)

  - 10-year explicit forecast: FY2026-FY2035
  - SBC-adjusted FCF as the underwriting column
    SBC-adj FCF = Adj FCF - Total SBC
    This treats stock comp as a real cash cost to all-cash investors,
    which is conservative and Buffett-appropriate.
  - Both perpetuity-growth and exit-multiple terminal values built
    separately, then averaged for reported IV.
  - Three scenarios: Bear (25%) / Base (55%) / Bull (20%)
  - Hurdle rates tested: 10%, 12%, 14%
    Primary: 10% (quality, $1.67B net cash) and 12% (mid-cap, narrow moat)
  - Buyback modeled explicitly via declining share count (-3% bear,
    -4% base, -5% bull annually -- net of SBC dilution).
  - Per-share IV is the reportable number, not enterprise IV.

  1.3  WHY SBC-ADJ FCF (NOT GAAP FCF)

  Treating SBC as free is a SaaS-industry convention that has cost
  long-term holders billions across the cycle. We deduct full SBC
  ($177M FY25, 14% of revenue) so that the underwriting reflects
  cash-equivalent return to a single-shareholder owner.

  Note: management is starting to offset SBC with buybacks (diluted
  share count flat YoY in Q4 2025). We model that offset SEPARATELY
  in the share-count line; we do NOT double-count by also lowering
  SBC.

  1.4  WHY 10% AND 12% AS PRIMARY HURDLES

  - 10% reflects MNDY's quality features: $1.67B net cash (zero debt
    risk), 89% gross margin, 27% trailing FCF margin, founder-led,
    large-customer NDR 116%.
  - 12% reflects MNDY's risk features: mid-cap ($3.4B mkt cap),
    NARROW moat (per deep dive), Microsoft Loop bundling threat,
    AI competitive risk, Israeli FPI governance discount.
  - 14% is included as a stress test for "what if everything goes
    against us simultaneously."

  1.5  TERMINAL VALUE LOGIC

  Two TV approaches run independently, then averaged:

  (a) PERPETUITY-GROWTH TV
      TV = FCF(year 11) / (r - g)
      where g = 3.0% (slightly above LT inflation; appropriate for
      a mature SaaS with continued international expansion runway)

  (b) EXIT-MULTIPLE TV
      TV = SBC-adj FCF(year 10) x exit multiple
      Bear:  14x (bear-case mature SaaS w/ no competitive edge)
      Base:  18x (peer-cohort steady-state)
      Bull:  22x (premium for AI agent platform optionality)

  Averaging the two methods is a discipline against either model's
  weakness: perpetuity-growth is sensitive to (r - g) tail; exit
  multiple is sensitive to comparables in a 10-year future state.
