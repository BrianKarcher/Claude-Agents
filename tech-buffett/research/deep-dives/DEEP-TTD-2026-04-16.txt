0. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

TTD earns a platform fee on roughly $12B+ of annual advertiser gross spend,
converting ~28% of revenue to free cash flow with a net-cash balance sheet and a
>95% customer retention rate. At ~$21 the market capitalizes ~$9.9B of equity
against $796M of 2025 FCF — ~12.4x trailing FCF — and prices in zero net growth
in perpetuity.

The bear case rests on four related pillars: (i) CFO instability signals
dysfunction and financial-control risk; (ii) Amazon DSP is a structurally
superior competitor that will compress TTD's take-rate and share; (iii) Ventura
OS is a capital-consuming side-bet that is quietly failing; (iv) the
deceleration from ~27% to ~10% growth is structural, not cyclical, and the new
normal is a 6-10% grower.

Our tentative view after this deep dive: pillars (i) and (iii) are real but
overblown; pillar (ii) is the single most important variable and is partially
priced in; pillar (iv) is probably 60% cyclical / 40% structural, which is
very different from the 100% structural outcome the stock implies. The founder's
$148M personal open-market purchase at prices 12-19% above current is a
high-signal corroboration that insiders see a cyclical dislocation, not a dying
franchise.

We remain on WATCH/ACCUMULATE and will make the buy/pass decision once a DCF is
built with (a) a take-rate compression scenario, (b) a Ventura-zero scenario,
and (c) Q1 2026 results on May 13 to validate or reject the 10% guidance floor.

1. UNIT ECONOMICS — WHAT TTD ACTUALLY SELLS
================================================================================

TTD is not an ad network. It does not buy or own media inventory. It operates a
cloud-based media trading platform, and it earns a platform fee on gross
advertiser spend. This is the "toll-road" characterization that matters for
every downstream question in this deep dive.

GROSS SPEND FLOW
-----------------
Advertiser allocates budget -> platform fee skimmed by TTD -> residual passes
through to ad exchanges / SSPs / publishers / data vendors. TTD does not
recognize gross spend as revenue — only the platform fee.

REVENUE = GROSS SPEND x TAKE RATE

FY2025 revenue of $2,896M at an approximate ~22-24% effective take rate on
gross spend implies roughly $12-13B of annual gross spend flowing through the
platform. Management has historically described the take rate as "~20%" on
smaller accounts and lower on large holding-company book-of-business deals.

CONTRIBUTION MARGIN AT THE DEAL LEVEL
--------------------------------------
Once a large agency or brand is integrated, incremental revenue on that account
carries a gross margin north of 80%. The platform is a fixed-cost technology
asset; incremental bid requests cost near-zero. This is why TTD's operating
leverage is so pronounced — FY2025 Adj. EBITDA margin was ~24%, Q4 2025 hit
~47%, and FCF margin was 27.5%.

COST STRUCTURE
---------------
- Platform operations / AWS: low-teens % of revenue, well understood
- Sales & marketing: ~21% — the largest line item and the key lever in a slow-
  growth environment. Management has room to flex this lower if growth stays
  muted.
- R&D: ~18%, funding Kokai, UID2, Ventura, Retail Media
- G&A: ~6%
- SBC: ~$300M+ annualized — genuinely large; we reconcile this below.

STOCK-BASED COMPENSATION RECONCILIATION
----------------------------------------
2025 SBC ran roughly $300-350M against $796M reported FCF. If we treat SBC as a
real economic cost (which Buffett and Munger both would), cash FCF net of SBC
is closer to $460-495M, or ~16-17% of revenue. That still yields a P/(cash FCF
net of SBC) of ~20-22x at $21 — not dirt cheap, but reasonable for a durable
platform.

We will build the DCF in two columns: reported FCF, and FCF-less-SBC. Any buy
must be underwritten on the more conservative column.

WHY THE BUSINESS DOES NOT COMMODITIZE EASILY
---------------------------------------------
A DSP is a network-effect business layered on a data-quality business layered
on a trust-and-transparency business. New advertiser spend improves Koa's AI
bidding, which improves outcomes, which attracts more advertisers. More
publisher integrations yield more inventory, which improves fill and price,
which attracts more advertisers. UID2 layers a third dimension of network value.

A challenger who started today would need:
  - petabyte-scale historical bid data to train a competitive AI bidder
  - integrations with ~100 major SSPs, data vendors, and measurement partners
  - 10,000+ trained agency traders who prefer their UI
  - trust of at least 5 of the 6 global holding companies

This is a ~5-year, multi-billion-dollar catch-up for any would-be challenger
that does not already have a distribution advantage. Only three companies have
that distribution advantage: Google, Amazon, and (plausibly) Meta.

2. TOTAL ADDRESSABLE MARKET — 10-YEAR VIEW
================================================================================

GLOBAL DIGITAL AD MARKET
-------------------------
2025 global digital advertising: ~$730B
2030 estimate: ~$1.1T (roughly 8-9% CAGR)

OF THAT, THE PROGRAMMATIC PORTION
----------------------------------
~$420B programmatic in 2025, growing to an estimated ~$800B by 2030.

OF THAT, THE OPEN-INTERNET PORTION (TTD'S HUNTING GROUND)
----------------------------------------------------------
Walled gardens (Google Search, Meta, TikTok, Amazon Ads O&O) capture ~65% of
programmatic. The "open internet" — premium CTV publishers, audio, digital out
of home, open-web display and video — is the remaining ~35%, or about $150B
today, growing to an estimated $280-320B by 2030. That is TTD's real TAM.

OF THAT, CONNECTED TV (CTV) IS THE GROWTH ENGINE
-------------------------------------------------
CTV spending is expected to exceed 40% of total global ad spend by 2030, with
US CTV alone at ~$51B by 2029 (from ~$30B in 2025). Industry expectations put
60-75% of US TV ad spend through a programmatic path by 2030, with essentially
all CTV automated in some form.

CTV is ~50% of TTD's current revenue mix. A 10-12% CAGR in US CTV plus faster
international growth over the next 5 years gives TTD a structural tailwind
distinct from macro cyclicality.

RETAIL MEDIA
-------------
Retail media globally is the fastest-growing ad segment — ~20% CAGR — and TTD's
Walmart, Kroger, and international retailer integrations position it as the
neutral demand-side intermediary for retail media budgets that do not flow
through Amazon's closed loop.

TTD PENETRATION
----------------
TTD's ~$12-13B gross spend today against a $150B open-internet programmatic TAM
is ~8% penetration. Even at moderate share gains and a flat TAM-to-TAM take
rate, the mathematical runway for decade-horizon compounding is intact. The
debate is not "is the TAM there" — it clearly is. The debate is "does TTD
capture its fair share of the incremental TAM, or does Amazon DSP and DV360
capture a disproportionate share at TTD's expense."

3. COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS — THE 5-10 YEAR VIEW
================================================================================

THE THREE-HORSE RACE
---------------------
Feb 2025 market share snapshot:
  Google DV360:       ~47%
  Amazon DSP:         ~20%
  The Trade Desk:     ~19%
  Everyone else:      ~14% (Xandr, Basis, MediaMath, agency-proprietary)

Amazon DSP grew ad revenue 23% YoY in Q4 2025 while TTD grew 14%. DV360 grew
mid-teens. That is a meaningful signal: Amazon is taking roughly 1-2 points of
share per year from the combined pie, and most of that is coming from TTD
rather than DV360 (because TTD and Amazon compete for the same open-internet
+ CTV budgets that bypass Google's owned-and-operated stack).

This is the bear case's strongest pillar, and it is real.

AMAZON DSP — STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------
1. First-party retail data on 200M+ Prime members — unreplicable
2. Prime Video CTV inventory owned-and-operated — subsidized placement
3. Netflix partnership (Q4 2025 onward) — Amazon DSP is the primary
   programmatic buyer for Netflix's ad tier in 12 markets including US, UK, JP
4. Yahoo DSP as a secondary partner (announced Mar 2026)
5. Cross-subsidy from AWS and Retail — Amazon can run the DSP as a loss leader
6. Spotify partnership (Fall 2025)

In short: Amazon has every structural advantage TTD does not have, and every
advantage it has (logged-in-shopper data, CTV inventory, cross-product pull)
maps directly onto the buckets where TTD's growth has slowed. The Netflix win
is the canary — Netflix is ~10% of global US ad-supported streaming hours, and
its primary DSP being Amazon is a real loss for TTD.

AMAZON DSP — STRUCTURAL DISADVANTAGES
--------------------------------------
1. Trust deficit with agencies — buying media through a retailer that competes
   with your brand's D2C strategy is a recurring objection (most CPG / apparel
   / consumer electronics advertisers face this tension)
2. Walled-garden architecture — Amazon DSP does not buy much open-internet
   inventory outside Amazon's o&o properties; its "off-Amazon" inventory
   quality has been criticized
3. Audit / transparency: Amazon does not offer full log-level data the way TTD
   does. Holding companies are uncomfortable running audit trails through a
   direct competitor.
4. Take rate opacity: Amazon's fee structure is notoriously opaque
5. No open identity standard — Amazon is a closed ID ecosystem; UID2 is the
   open-internet alternative that TTD maintains

THE REAL COMPETITIVE QUESTION
------------------------------
It is not "Amazon vs TTD for the whole TAM." It is "Amazon vs TTD for the
fastest-growing CTV + retail-adjacent incremental dollars." Our view:
  - Amazon will continue to win disproportionate share of CTV for the next 3-5
    years, particularly inventory tied to their retail loop
  - TTD will retain dominant share of the non-Amazon open internet, CTV ex-
    Prime+Netflix, audio, and the identity/measurement layer
  - Take rates will compress 100-200 bps across the industry as Amazon pushes
    aggressive pricing, hitting TTD disproportionately

A 150 bps permanent take-rate compression on TTD's base applies ~6-7% revenue
impact. That's painful but absorbable. The DCF will model this explicitly.

GOOGLE DV360
-------------
Google's DSP is not going away, but its structural advantage is under
unprecedented assault. The DOJ antitrust case against Google's ad-tech stack
resulted in liability findings in 2024-2025 and remedy proceedings are ongoing
through 2026. Potential outcomes range from behavioral remedies (low harm to
Google) to forced divestiture of AdX or parts of DV360 (significant tailwind
for TTD as open-internet inventory would be forced into a more competitive
auction). We model DV360 as a neutral-to-positive optionality variable.

META AND TIKTOK
----------------
These are walled gardens on the sell side. They are competitors for ad budget,
not DSP share. The risk here is secular, not structural to TTD: when macro
softens, CPG and auto pull budget back into Meta and TikTok because the
conversion attribution is simpler, even if the ROAS is worse long-term. This is
part of the cyclical explanation for the 2026 deceleration.

4. THE BEAR CASE — DETAILED TEARDOWN
================================================================================

This section is the core of this deep dive. The user has specifically asked us
to stress-test four bear-case pillars. We take each in turn and attempt to
reach a defensible probabilistic verdict.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BEAR PILLAR 1: CFO INSTABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FACTS
------
- Laura Schenkein served as CFO ~June 2023 to mid-2025 (~2 years). Departure
  was an orderly, announced transition.
- Alex Kayyal appointed CFO August 2025. Former Lightspeed Venture Partners GP.
  Kept a board seat upon appointment.
- Kayyal was TERMINATED (company's own word) effective January 24, 2026 —
  after just five months. The termination was announced on a Sunday.
- Tahnil Davis, 11-year company veteran and Chief Accounting Officer, named
  interim CFO. Chief Accounting Officer titles typically imply deep knowledge
  of the books.
- At the time of termination, TTD simultaneously REAFFIRMED its 2025 results
  (subsequently confirmed at reported numbers) and its forward guidance.
- Kayyal retained his board seat through the 2026 annual meeting — suggesting
  a governance dispute rather than a financial-control malfeasance.
- Jeff Green bought $148M of stock personally on March 2-4, 2026, at $23-25.
  A CEO does not put $148M of personal capital behind a company where he
  believes books are compromised.

INTERPRETATION
---------------
Two CFO transitions in 30 months is a governance yellow flag, not a red flag,
under three conditions that appear to be met:
  1. The reaffirmation of 2025 results — if there were an accounting problem,
     this would not have happened
  2. The interim CFO is a deep insider with a long clean record
  3. The CEO's personal open-market buying at materially higher prices

The most parsimonious explanation is a culture/operating fit issue between Green
and Kayyal (Kayyal was a VC-background outsider landing in a founder-led,
operator-driven culture), not a financial-control or strategic crisis. Kayyal's
continued board seat reinforces this.

RESIDUAL RISK
--------------
If the permanent CFO hire is delayed past Q2 2026, or if the Q1 2026 report on
May 13 contains a restatement, the interpretation changes dramatically.

VERDICT
--------
Bear pillar 1: 25% weight. Real concern, partially visible, fully priced in.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BEAR PILLAR 2: AMAZON DSP COMPETITION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FACTS
------
- Amazon ad revenue grew 23% in Q4 2025 vs TTD's 14%
- Amazon DSP is now ~20% DSP market share vs TTD ~19%
- Netflix programmatic primary partner = Amazon DSP (Q4 2025 onward)
- Spotify programmatic primary partner (fall 2025) = Amazon DSP
- Amazon is subsidizing the DSP, per multiple analyst reports
- Prime-shopper first-party data remains a structural Amazon advantage

INTERPRETATION
---------------
The Netflix and Spotify losses are concrete and matter. However, Amazon's
share gains have so far come disproportionately from the long tail of small
DSPs and from budget expansion by advertisers who were not previously
programmatic at all (bodega brands using Amazon Ads for the first time), not
from TTD's top-200 agency accounts. TTD's >95% gross retention through the
Amazon ramp-up is hard evidence of this. If Amazon were stealing core TTD
customers, retention would already be below 95%.

The structural asymmetry: TTD's average enterprise customer spends across 50+
supply partners. Amazon DSP customers concentrate spend on Amazon o&o + a thin
layer of third-party inventory. These are, in practice, two different use
cases. They overlap at the margin on CTV and on lower-funnel conversion
campaigns. They do NOT overlap on brand-building, upper-funnel reach, open-
internet video, audio, DOOH, or international.

The most likely 5-year outcome is a splintering of the DSP market into:
  - Amazon DSP: retail-connected, CTV owned-and-operated, mid-market brands
  - TTD: open-internet premium brand + agency workflow, global reach, ID graph
  - DV360: Google-stack-adjacent buyers, YouTube-anchored
  - Long tail: decline

In that world TTD can still compound at 12-16% for a decade — just not 25-30%.

RESIDUAL RISK
--------------
If Amazon begins winning anchor holding-company contracts (WPP GroupM, Omnicom
OMG) as primary rather than secondary DSP, the thesis breaks. Watch WPP and
Omnicom DSP preferred-partner announcements in 2026-2027.

Separately, if Amazon slashes its effective take rate to compete with TTD on
a cost basis, TTD take rates compress industry-wide. We model a 150 bps take-
rate compression in the DCF base case.

VERDICT
--------
Bear pillar 2: the single most important variable in the thesis. 35% weight.
Partially priced in at current ~$21 price but not fully. Outcome bifurcates on
holding-company preferred-partner decisions in the next 24 months.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BEAR PILLAR 3: VENTURA OS EXECUTION RISK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FACTS
------
- Ventura announced November 2024 as a proprietary CTV operating system
- Sonos hardware partnership (rumored) fell through
- Only announced OEM at launch: DirecTV custom UI (a satellite/streaming
  hybrid, not primary TV market)
- February 2026: pivoted from "standalone OS" to "Ventura Ecosystem" with VIDAA
  (V, 50M+ Hisense/Toshiba devices) and Nexxen
- No published unit-economics or device-shipment guidance
- Capex and R&D cost for Ventura is commingled with Kokai and platform
  development — not separately disclosed

INTERPRETATION
---------------
Ventura OS as originally conceived — a challenger to Roku and Fire TV on a
hardware-by-hardware basis — was always a low-probability moonshot. The
pivoting to an "ecosystem" / integration layer approach in Feb 2026 is the
right call made a few quarters too late. It essentially concedes that TTD will
not build a Roku competitor and instead focuses on making existing TV OSes
more programmatic-friendly.

The VIDAA partnership matters: VIDAA powers Hisense and Toshiba smart TVs, which
together ship ~12-15M units per year in the US and 50M+ globally. If Ventura's
ad stack becomes the default programmatic plumbing on those devices, it is a
meaningful footprint — not Roku-scale, but meaningful.

Nexxen is a supply-side platform. The Nexxen partnership gives Ventura a
direct inventory pipeline without needing OEM deals for every inventory source.

KEY QUESTION: How much of TTD's ~$400M of annualized R&D went to Ventura vs
Kokai? Management has not broken this out. If Ventura consumed >$100M of 2025
R&D and still has not produced material revenue, it has been value-destructive.

BULL READING
-------------
Ventura was framed by Green as a strategic option to ensure the CTV supply
chain stays open. Even if Ventura OS itself never scales, the threat of it
existing may have been sufficient to extract better terms from Roku, Samsung
Tizen, Vizio, etc., during 2024-2025 inventory negotiations. Option value has
been partially harvested.

VERDICT
--------
Bear pillar 3: 10% weight. Real cost center, not a company-killer. The
ecosystem pivot suggests management self-corrected. The project should be
sized in the DCF as ~$100M sunk cost with negligible 5-year revenue
contribution in the base case, and meaningful upside (~$200-300M high-margin
revenue) in the bull case.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BEAR PILLAR 4: CYCLICAL vs STRUCTURAL DECELERATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the most important debate for the valuation.

FACTS
------
Revenue growth trajectory:
  FY2023: +23%
  FY2024: +26% (benefit from US election political ad spend)
  FY2025: +18%
  Q1 2025: +25%
  Q2 2025: +19%
  Q3 2025: +18%
  Q4 2025: +14% (reported), ~+19% ex-political base effect
  Q1 2026 guide: +10% ("at least $678M")

CYCLICAL ARGUMENTS
-------------------
1. 2024 was an unusually strong political ad year — ~$200M+ of political ad
   spend flowed through TTD in 2024, compressing 2025 comparisons. Ex-
   political, Q4 2025 growth was ~19%, not 14%. This is a known base effect.
2. CPG and auto (~25-30% of TTD vertical exposure) cut budgets aggressively
   in H2 2025 on tariff/inflation uncertainty. Agencies are releasing budgets
   quarter-by-quarter rather than annually.
3. Kokai transition: forcing a full UI migration in 2025 required agencies to
   retrain traders and workflows. Adoption friction reduced near-term spend
   but sets up multi-year improvement — 26% better CPA, 58% better cost-per-
   unique-reach, 94% higher CTR per company-reported case studies.
4. Publicis memo disrupted ~$300-500M of annualized spend while transparency
   audit ran — a one-time event rather than a run-rate impairment. Publicis
   on their own Q1 2026 earnings call confirmed they have no intention to
   build a competing offer.

STRUCTURAL ARGUMENTS
---------------------
1. Amazon DSP is taking disproportionate share of the fastest-growing pool
   (CTV + retail-connected budgets)
2. AI-driven walled-garden ads (Meta Advantage+, Google Performance Max) are
   winning direct-response budget that used to test on TTD
3. Take rates are under durable compression pressure as Amazon subsidizes
4. Open-web supply is shrinking in some verticals as AI search (Google AI
   Overviews, ChatGPT) reduces publisher page views, compressing the open-
   internet impression pool
5. Mature-market CTV penetration (US) is plateauing; incremental growth
   requires international expansion, where TTD is weaker

OUR PROBABILISTIC DECOMPOSITION
--------------------------------
Applying a rough attribution to the deceleration from the 27% FY2024 growth
rate to the 10% Q1 2026 guide (17-point gap):

  Political base effect (2024 election lap):     ~3-4 points  (cyclical)
  Macro/tariff CPG+auto budget cuts:              ~4-5 points  (cyclical)
  Kokai migration friction:                       ~2 points    (transitional)
  Publicis audit disruption:                      ~1-2 points  (one-time)
  Amazon DSP share erosion on CTV:                ~3-4 points  (structural)
  Take-rate compression:                          ~1-2 points  (structural)

That attribution gives roughly 10-12 points of transient (cyclical + one-time +
transitional) deceleration and 4-6 points of genuine structural deceleration.

Reading that forward, a "normalized" 2027 growth rate — once political effects,
Kokai friction, and Publicis audit are lapped, and assuming macro CPG+auto
normalizes — should be in the 14-18% range, not the 10% implied by Q1 2026.

If that attribution is correct, the current valuation is substantially too
punitive. If Amazon's share erosion is larger than we estimate (e.g., 8-10
points structural rather than 4-6), then 10% becomes the new normal and the
stock is roughly fairly priced at $21.

VERDICT
--------
Bear pillar 4: the deceleration is probably ~60% cyclical, ~40% structural.
The market is pricing closer to 100% structural. This is the key disagreement
between our view and the consensus, and it is where DCF sensitivity analysis
will earn its keep.

5. BULL CASE — WHAT COULD GO RIGHT OVER 5-10 YEARS
================================================================================

CATALYST 1: CYCLICAL NORMALIZATION (2026-2027)
-----------------------------------------------
CPG and auto budgets rebound as tariff uncertainty clears; political ad spend
returns in 2028 (midterm year); Kokai adoption stabilizes into measurable ROI
improvement. Growth reaccelerates to 15-20%.

CATALYST 2: GOOGLE ANTITRUST REMEDIES (2026-2028)
--------------------------------------------------
Remedy decisions in the Google ad-tech case arrive in 2026-2027. Any remedy
that separates AdX or DV360 from Google's owned-and-operated stack increases
the open-internet pool that TTD buys from. In the favorable-remedy scenario,
TTD captures 3-5 points of incremental share without lifting a finger.

CATALYST 3: UID2 BECOMES DEFAULT OPEN-INTERNET ID (2026-2028)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Third-party cookies are gone. UID2 is the most-adopted non-walled-garden ID.
If UID2 reaches critical mass on the supply side, TTD sits at the center of
the identity graph and commands a premium take rate on UID2-enabled inventory
(2.9x CTR, 2.4x CPA per TTD case studies).

CATALYST 4: CTV SECULAR GROWTH THROUGH 2030
--------------------------------------------
CTV ad spend reaches ~$51B US by 2029, ~40% of global ad spend by 2030. Even
losing some share to Amazon, the absolute dollar flow through TTD's CTV book
can compound at 12-16% annually through 2030.

CATALYST 5: KOKAI MARGIN EXPANSION
-----------------------------------
Mature Kokai fully eliminates low-value labor across the buying workflow.
Operating leverage from 24% Adj. EBITDA margin in 2025 to 30-35% by 2029 on
even modest revenue growth yields substantial FCF growth.

CATALYST 6: BALANCE SHEET AND CAPITAL RETURN
---------------------------------------------
~$1.7B cash/ST investments and no debt. Modest growth investment requirement.
Rising surplus cash flow gives management multiple paths: buybacks at current
trough multiples are highly accretive; tuck-in M&A for measurement/data
capabilities; continued investment in Kokai/UID2. Management has not yet
announced a formal buyback authorization — doing so would be a significant
signal.

BULL CASE 10-YEAR FINANCIAL PICTURE
------------------------------------
Revenue: $2.9B (2025) -> $7-8B (2030) -> $11-13B (2035) at 12-14% CAGR
EBITDA margin: 24% -> 32% -> 35%
FCF: $800M -> $2.0B -> $3.5B
At a fair 20x exit multiple on $2.0B of 2030 FCF = $40B enterprise value
(~4x today's $10B market cap, ~15% annual return plus cash accumulation).

7. OPTIONALITY
================================================================================

TTD has three distinct optionality vectors that would each be worth
incremental value if they succeeded:

(A) UID2 AS INDUSTRY STANDARD
------------------------------
If UID2 becomes the default identity spine for the non-walled-garden internet,
TTD captures economics across every impression transacted on UID2 — including
impressions not bought through TTD's DSP. This would create a true neutral-
infrastructure layer similar to how Visa captures economics on every Visa
transaction regardless of which bank issued the card. We do not underwrite
this optionality in the base case.

(B) VENTURA ECOSYSTEM BECOMES THE "OPEN CTV PROGRAMMATIC LAYER"
----------------------------------------------------------------
Ecosystem pivot with VIDAA/Nexxen could make Ventura the default programmatic
plumbing for non-Roku, non-Amazon CTV devices. ~$200-300M of high-margin
revenue by 2029 in the bull outcome. Not underwritten in the base case.

(C) INTERNATIONAL EXPANSION
----------------------------
TTD revenue is still ~88% North America. International is growing faster than
US but off a small base. Capturing 10% of EMEA + APAC open-internet
programmatic by 2030 would add $1-2B of revenue. Partially underwritten in the
base case.

(D) DOJ GOOGLE REMEDY WINDFALL
-------------------------------
If AdX/DV360 is forced to divest, TTD is the single largest beneficiary. This
is a material, asymmetric put option embedded in the stock for free at current
prices. Not underwritten in the base case.
