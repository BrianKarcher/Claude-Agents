0.  EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

The bear thesis on MNDY is essentially: "Work-management is being commoditized,
the FY2026 guide-down is the first crack of structural deceleration, and at
$65 you have a melting ice cube with $32/share of cash."

The bull thesis is: "MNDY is a 20%+ grower with 89% gross margins, 26%
trailing FCF margins, $32/share of net cash, and a multi-product platform
that just hit AI inflection -- trading at 6x EV/FCF, the lowest valuation
since IPO. The FY2026 guide is FX + a deliberate one-year investment
cycle, not the start of a permanent decline."

After working through the pillars below, my probability-weighted view is:

    Bear (structural)         25%
    Base (cyclical + FX)      55%
    Bull (re-acceleration)    20%

10-year IRRs from $65.32 (preliminary, to be refined in DCF):

    Bear case:    ~3-5% (mostly cash floor)
    Base case:    ~17-21% (the heart of the thesis)
    Bull case:    ~28-35%
    Prob-wtd:     ~17%

Five-year EV per share (preliminary):

    Bear:  ~$78  (+19% from $65.32)
    Base:  ~$165 (+153%)
    Bull:  ~$310 (+374%)
    Prob-wtd: ~$170 (+160% over 5 yrs ~ 21% IRR)

The asymmetry is the central feature of the setup: even in the structural
bear case, $32/share of cash + 19% FCF margins + slow growth produces a
floor near today's price. The downside is the time-cost of capital, not
permanent loss.

CONDITIONS THAT MUST HOLD FOR THE THESIS:
  1. FY2026 revenue growth >=18% (i.e., guide is met)
  2. FY2027 revenue growth bottoms at >=15% (no further deceleration)
  3. NDR >$100K stays >=115% over the next 4 quarters
  4. Multi-product attach rate among >$50K customers reaches >=35% by YE 2027
  5. Buyback continues at current pace, retiring >=5% of float per year
     while stock remains below $100
  6. No Microsoft Loop announcement that bundles a fully-functional Work OS
     into M365 base SKUs (i.e. without an upcharge)
  7. AI products (sidekick / vibe / agents / workflows) reach >=$100M ARR
     by YE 2027

If 5 of 7 hold, the base case is likely. If <=3 hold, the thesis is broken
and we should not own.

1.  UNIT ECONOMICS DEEP DIVE
================================================================================

  1.1  REVENUE PER CUSTOMER (the most important unit metric)

  +-------------------------+--------+--------+--------+
  | Metric                  | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
  +-------------------------+--------+--------+--------+
  | Revenue ($M)            | 1,232  | 972    | 730    |
  | Paid >10-user customers | 63,914 | 59,214 | 53,889 |
  | Implied ARPC ($)        | 19,275 | 16,415 | 13,545 |
  | ARPC YoY                | +17%   | +21%   | --     |
  | YoY rev growth          | +27%   | +33%   | +41%   |
  | YoY customer growth     | +8%    | +10%   | --     |
  +-------------------------+--------+--------+--------+

  The deceleration in revenue growth (41% -> 33% -> 27%) maps almost
  perfectly onto the deceleration in customer growth (10% -> 8% combined
  with ARPC growth from 21% -> 17%). Roughly 60% of the deceleration is
  customer-count slowdown; 40% is ARPC growth slowdown. Critically, ARPC
  is still growing 17% -- meaning the base is becoming higher-quality
  even as it grows more slowly.

  1.2  COHORT NDR DECOMPOSITION

  Blended NDR has drifted: 112% -> 110%. But within that:

    >$100K customers:  116% -> 116% (flat, healthy)
    >$50K customers:   115% -> 116% (modestly improving)
    All customers:     112% -> 110% (-200 bps, weaker)

  The math implies the SMB / sub-$50K cohort NDR is dropping faster than
  the blend implies. Plausible drivers: (a) 2024 price increase anniversary
  (mechanical), (b) macro pressure on small accounts, (c) competitive
  churn to ClickUp/Notion at the low end. Of these, only (c) is a
  long-term concern.

  Reading: SMB churn is real but small dollars; large-customer expansion
  is healthy and stable. This is consistent with a "moving upmarket"
  story, not "structural deceleration." For a long-term investor it
  is the >$100K NDR that matters most -- and it is rock solid at 116%.

  1.3  GROSS MARGIN AND CONTRIBUTION ECONOMICS

  Gross margin has been rock-stable at 89% for three years. This is
  upper-decile SaaS gross margin. Because the COGS line scales sub-
  linearly with customers (cloud infrastructure dominates), there is no
  visible pricing pressure forcing GM lower. This is a critical signal
  -- if the category were truly commoditizing, we would see GM compression
  first. We do not.

  1.4  CAC PAYBACK AND S&M EFFICIENCY

  Sales & Marketing as % of revenue:
                    FY2023:   60%
                    FY2024:   55%
                    FY2025:   51%

  Sales efficiency, measured as new ARR / S&M spend, has improved from
  ~0.7 to ~0.85 over three years. This is the inverse of what we'd see
  in a structurally decelerating SaaS company. On a Magic Number basis,
  monday.com is around 0.7-0.8 -- consistent with healthy LTM growth
  and a still-ramping enterprise GTM.

  CAC payback period (estimated): ~16-20 months on a fully-loaded basis,
  ~10-12 months on a contribution basis. This is acceptable for SMB-
  weighted SaaS but not industry-leading. Enterprise accounts pay back
  faster (~10-15 months) and are growing faster, so the trajectory is
  improving.

  1.5  RULE OF 40 AND QUALITY OF GROWTH

  +-------+--------+--------+--------+--------+---------+
  | Year  | Rev gr | NG OpM | R40    | FCF M  | R40-FCF |
  +-------+--------+--------+--------+--------+---------+
  | 2023  | +41%   | 8%     | 49     | 28%    | 69      |
  | 2024  | +33%   | 14%    | 47     | 30%    | 63      |
  | 2025  | +27%   | 14%    | 41     | 26%    | 53      |
  | 2026E | +18%   | 11%    | 29     | 19%    | 37      |
  +-------+--------+--------+--------+--------+---------+

  Rule of 40 score deteriorating from 69 -> 53 -> 37 (FCF basis). At 37
  the company is "below R40," which historically maps to mid-teens
  EV/sales multiples in normal markets. MNDY is at 1.4-1.6x EV/sales,
  i.e. ~10x below normal R40 implied range. The market is pricing in
  *further* compression to R40 ~25 (i.e. 15% growth + 10% FCF margin)
  -- a deeply pessimistic assumption.

  Question: how plausible is recovery to R40 = 45-50 by FY2028?
  Answer: requires either (a) growth re-acceleration to 22%+ or (b) FCF
  margin recovery to 25%+. We need to make either case in section 4.

  +------------------------------------+---------+---------+---------+
  | Realistic addressable for MNDY     | ~$50B   | ~$120B  | 19%     |
  +------------------------------------+---------+---------+---------+

  monday.com FY2025 revenue of $1.23B = ~2.5% of realistic addressable.
  At base-case 14% revenue CAGR over 10 years, FY2035 revenue would be
  ~$4.6B -- still only 4% of the 2030 TAM and a smaller share of 2035
  TAM (assumed to grow further). Penetration is the constraint, not TAM.

  The "AI agents for work" category is the swing factor. monday.com's
  vibe + agents + workflows + sidekick suite is positioned to take a
  meaningful slice if execution holds. A 5% share of a $40B 2030 AI
  agents-for-work TAM would be $2B of incremental revenue -- 40% of
  current trailing revenue. This is the bull-case re-acceleration
  vector.

  TAM HEADWINDS:
  - Microsoft M365 has 400M+ paid commercial seats. Loop / Planner /
    Project / Power Platform compete in the same submarkets but are
    bundled. If Microsoft chooses to make Loop genuinely good and
    bundled (unclear today), the SMB sub-segment of MNDY's TAM
    shrinks materially.
  - Salesforce + Slack bundling does similar damage in the CRM-adjacent
    segment.
  - AI-native upstarts (Linear, Notion, ClickUp) take share at the low
    end. ClickUp has reportedly hit ~$500M ARR.

  TAM TAILWINDS:
  - "Vibe coding" and AI agents create a *new* category that is
    add-on revenue to the existing Work OS, not cannibalistic.
  - Hybrid + remote work continues to drive demand for collaborative
    work-tracking platforms.
  - International digitalization (Europe, LatAm, APAC) is in early
    innings -- monday.com has strong non-US presence as a non-US
    company.

  Reading: the TAM is large enough to support 14% CAGR for 10 years if
  market share stays flat. The bull case requires share gains in both
  enterprise (vs. ServiceNow / Asana / Atlassian) and AI-agents (a
  greenfield category). The bear case requires share losses to Microsoft
  bundling -- which has been threatened for 4 years and has not
  materialized.

3.  COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS OVER 5-10 YEARS
================================================================================

  3.1  THE MICROSOFT QUESTION (the single biggest variable)

  Microsoft has 4 products in the Work OS adjacency:
    - Microsoft Planner (free / bundled): basic task lists
    - Microsoft Project (paid premium): traditional PPM
    - Microsoft Loop (bundled into M365): real-time collaborative docs
    - Microsoft Power Platform (paid premium): low-code automations

  Loop is the most existential threat. Launched 2023, GA in 2024, still
  not feature-equivalent to monday.com or ClickUp as of early 2026.
  Microsoft's pattern is to underinvest in horizontal collaboration
  products (Yammer, Teams Connect, Loop) until competitive pressure
  forces a sprint. The pattern with Slack -> Teams suggests Microsoft
  CAN execute when motivated; the pattern with Asana / monday.com
  suggests they have NOT been motivated yet.

  Why hasn't Microsoft prioritized Loop?
  - Teams is the strategic priority and is doing well; Loop competes
    indirectly with Teams Channels.
  - The work-management market is too small (~$14B) to move MSFT's
    needle ($300B+ revenue).
  - Microsoft's bundle creates customer lock-in regardless of Loop's
    quality -- they win even if customers also pay for monday.com.

  Probability Microsoft makes Loop a true monday.com-killer in 5 years:
  ~35% (medium-low; Microsoft has had 4 years to do it and hasn't).

  Probability Microsoft makes Loop "good enough" to suppress monday.com
  net new SMB customer growth: ~55% (medium-high; this is already
  partially happening).

  Probability Microsoft fully bundles a Work OS (no upcharge) into
  M365 base SKUs: ~15% (low; would cannibalize Project Plan SKUs).

  Net: Microsoft is a structural headwind worth 5-10% of monday.com's
  potential TAM, but not an existential threat unless something
  changes materially.

  3.2  THE SALESFORCE / SERVICENOW QUESTION

  Both are enterprise-only, both bundle adjacent capabilities, both
  have stronger enterprise GTM than monday.com. They are credible
  threats at the >$100K customer tier where monday.com is moving.

  However, both ServiceNow and Salesforce are priced at 8-15x revenue
  and are sold to CIO/CRO offices via long enterprise cycles. monday.com
  prices at $20-50/seat/month and lands bottom-up. The two motions
  rarely compete head-to-head.

  Net: SFDC + NOW take some net new enterprise wins from MNDY but
  also create a "best-of-breed" displacement opportunity for MNDY in
  accounts with SFDC bloat / NOW project management gaps. Net effect
  is roughly neutral.

  3.3  THE AI-NATIVE UPSTARTS (Notion, ClickUp, Linear, Airtable)

  These compete primarily at the SMB tier on price and AI-first design.
  ClickUp's price war (~50% lower than monday.com) is real and is
  likely the source of some of MNDY's SMB NDR softness.

  Long-term: these companies have not demonstrated they can move
  upmarket. Notion has $200-300M ARR at ~30% growth (slowing); Linear
  is ~$50M ARR (small); ClickUp may plateau as it discovers enterprise
  is expensive to serve. Each is a competitor for the SMB long-tail
  but not for the >$100K accounts where MNDY's revenue concentration is.

  Net: AI-native upstarts limit MNDY's SMB growth ceiling but are not
  a direct threat to the upmarket motion that drives 60%+ of MNDY's
  revenue growth.

  3.4  ASANA AS A WARNING (or as MNDY's reflection?)

  Asana is the cautionary tale: 9-12% growth, perpetual cash burn (until
  recently), losing share to monday.com. Asana's market cap is ~$3B;
  monday.com's is $3.3B. The market is pricing them similarly.

  But Asana grows at 10%; MNDY grows at 27%. Asana has 30% gross margin
  customer success burdens; MNDY has 89% GM. Asana's NDR has fallen
  below 100%; MNDY's is 110%. The market is pricing MNDY *as if* it is
  becoming Asana over the next 18-24 months -- which would be an
  unprecedented collapse for a company growing 27% with stable margins.

  3.5  TEN-YEAR COMPETITIVE OUTLOOK

  Most likely outcome: 2-4 winners share the work-management category.
  monday.com is positioned to be one of them, alongside Atlassian (dev),
  ServiceNow (ITSM), and one of [ClickUp / Notion] (SMB / consumerized).
  Microsoft Loop becomes a "good enough" bundled option that suppresses
  monday.com's SMB unit economics but does not displace its upmarket
  motion.

  In this most-likely outcome, monday.com:
    - Reaches $4-5B revenue by 2035 (12-14% CAGR)
    - 22-25% steady-state FCF margin
    - 20-25x exit FCF multiple
    - $80-110B EV by 2035, $100-200/share with buyback compounding

4.  THE FX VS. STRUCTURAL DECELERATION QUESTION
================================================================================

  This is the single most important section because the FY2026 guide
  is what crushed the stock 78%, and the question of whether FY2026 is
  "FX + investment cycle" or "structural" is the entire bull/bear axis.

  4.1  THE FY2026 GUIDE DECOMPOSED

  +----------------------------+---------+---------+---------+
  | Metric                     | FY2025A | FY2026E | Delta   |
  +----------------------------+---------+---------+---------+
  | Revenue growth (YoY)       | +27%    | +18-19% | -800bps |
  | Non-GAAP op margin         | 14%     | 11-12%  | -250bps |
  | Adj FCF margin             | 26%     | 19-20%  | -650bps |
  +----------------------------+---------+---------+---------+

  Management attributes the margin compression as:
    FX:           ~100-200 bps  (their disclosure)
    Investments:  ~150-200 bps  (R&D for AI products + GTM hires)
    Mix shift:    ~50-100 bps   (enterprise mix carries higher CAC)
    Other:        ~50-100 bps   (HQ build-out tail, CCO transition)

  The revenue growth deceleration of -800bps decomposes as:
    Cyclical / FX:    ~200-300 bps (USD strength, soft macro)
    Tough comp:       ~100-200 bps (FY2025's 27% was a strong comp)
    Structural:       ~300-400 bps (mature SaaS deceleration curve)
    Investment lag:   ~100 bps    (new sales hires take 6-12 months
                                    to ramp -- 2025 hires book in 2026)

  Reading: the deceleration is roughly 35% cyclical, 35% structural,
  and 30% transitory (comps + investment lag). At face value the bear
  is right that there's some structural deceleration, but the bull is
  also right that there's significant cyclical and transitory drag.

  4.2  HISTORICAL FX EXPOSURE

  The 20-F discloses ~50% of revenue is non-USD. Approximate billing
  currency mix:
    USD:   50%
    EUR:   18%
    GBP:   8%
    AUD:   5%
    Other: 19%

  USD/EUR has appreciated ~6% YoY into 2026; USD/GBP +4%; USD index
  up ~5% on a trade-weighted basis. Reported revenue from non-USD
  customers is therefore down ~250 bps just from FX. This squares
  with management's 100-200bps margin guide-down attribution.

  Critical: FX is mean-reverting over 3-5 year horizons. If USD
  weakens in 2027-2028 (consensus expects this), there will be a
  ~200-400bps reported revenue tailwind. Long-term investors should
  treat FX as noise, not signal.

  4.3  THE INVESTMENT CYCLE ARGUMENT

  In Q3 2024, the prior CRO left. In 2025, three new C-level GTM
  hires (CRO, CMO, CCO) joined. New sales leaders typically ramp
  for 4-6 quarters before their teams hit full productivity.

  Management has said publicly that 2026 is an "investment year" --
  meaning the deliberate ramping of sales/marketing capacity ahead
  of expected enterprise wins in 2027. This is exactly the same
  playbook ServiceNow used in 2014-2015 and Atlassian in 2018-2019:
  step-function investments produce flat-margin years that look bad
  on a P&L but produce 18-24 months later.

  If 2026 is genuinely an investment cycle (not a desperate response
  to deceleration), then 2027 should show:
    - Revenue growth re-accelerating to 20-22%
    - Margins recovering to 14-15%
    - FCF margin recovering to 22-24%

  The key tells:
    - >$500K customer growth must remain 60%+ YoY in 2026
    - Multi-product attach rate must reach 35%+ for >$50K customers
    - cRPO must keep growing >25%
    - vibe / agents / workflows ARR must reach $50M+ by YE 2026

  4.4  STRUCTURAL DECELERATION RISK SIZE

  If we are wrong and the deceleration is structural, the path is:
    FY2026: 19% growth (guide met)
    FY2027: 14-16% growth (further deceleration)
    FY2028: 11-13% growth
    FY2029: 9-11% growth
    Steady-state: 7-9% (Asana convergence)

  In this path, the Year-10 revenue = ~$2.7-3.0B with 20% FCF margins
  = $540-600M FCF. Exit multiple 14-16x = $7.5-9.5B EV. Plus net cash
  of $2-3B accumulated by Year 10 = equity value $10-12B = $190-230
  per share if buyback continues. Discounted to today at 12% = $61-74
  per share. Today is $65.32.

  Reading: even the structural-deceleration bear case puts a near-floor
  under the current price. This is the asymmetry. The downside is
  bounded by net-cash + a profitable underlying business; the upside
  requires execution but does not require unprecedented growth.

  4.5  PROBABILITY-WEIGHTED CONCLUSION

  +---------------------------+---------+---------+---------+
  | Scenario                  | Bear    | Base    | Bull    |
  +---------------------------+---------+---------+---------+
  | Probability               | 25%     | 55%     | 20%     |
  | FY2026 rev growth         | 17%     | 19%     | 21%     |
  | FY2027 rev growth         | 14%     | 19%     | 23%     |

5.  CAPITAL ALLOCATION DEEP DIVE
================================================================================

  5.1  HISTORICAL CAPITAL ALLOCATION

  monday.com's capital deployment 2021-2025:
    - IPO (June 2021): raised $574M
    - Total cash flow generated since IPO: ~$925M (op CF, cumulative)
    - Total acquisitions: ~$0M (no M&A)
    - Total dividends: $0
    - Total buybacks before 2025: $0
    - Total buybacks 2025: $135M (Q4 only, avg $152.74)
    - Total cash on balance sheet: $1.66B
    - Total foundation grants: $42M (1% of IPO proceeds + ongoing)

  This is the profile of management that hoarded cash for 4 years
  post-IPO before initiating any return of capital. This is deeply
  conservative and arguably suboptimal -- if they had repurchased
  $200M/year at sub-$200 prices in 2022-2024, EPS would be ~10-15%
  higher today.

  However, this conservatism also means:
    - No ill-advised M&A (very common SaaS mistake)
    - No promotional EPS engineering
    - Cash buffer through cycles (unusual for $1B SaaS)

  Capital allocation grade: B+ (operationally A-, slightly slow on
  buyback initiation, but no value-destroying M&A and well-timed
  capital return now that valuation supports it).

  5.2  BUYBACK ROIC ANALYSIS

  $870M authorization / $735M remaining as of Dec 31, 2025.

  Q4 2025 deployment: $135M at avg $152.74 = 883,913 shares.

  If management deploys the full remaining $735M in 2026-2027 at an
  average price of $80 (mix of pre-Q1 lower prices and post-Q1 higher),
  they would retire ~9.2M shares = 17% of float. EPS accretion would be
  ~17% over 18-24 months. This alone would justify a 15-20% stock
  re-rating.

  Buyback velocity test: Q4 2025 deployment of $135M was 1.0% of float
  (884K of 53M shares). If management maintains that pace ($135M/qtr),
  the program completes in 5-6 quarters. At today's $65 prices, that
  would retire ~11.3M shares = 21% of float in 18 months. This is
  *extremely* shareholder-friendly buyback velocity for a SaaS company.

  Question: how confident are we management actually deploys at this
  pace? Buyback authorization != buyback execution. The Q4 burst at
  $152 average suggests willingness when prices are "low" relative to
  history. At $65 they should be aggressive. Watch quarterly pacing
  in the Q1 2026 print.

6.  FOUNDER / MANAGEMENT QUALITY
================================================================================

  6.1  THE CO-CEO STRUCTURE

  Co-CEO structures fail more often than they succeed. Famous failures:
  Netflix (Hastings/Sarandos worked, then transitioned), Whole Foods
  (Mackey/Robb), Salesforce (Benioff/Bret Taylor briefly), SAP, Oracle.

  Famous successes (rare): Atlassian (Cannon-Brookes/Farquhar -- 16
  years and counting), Workday (Bhusri/Duffield -- worked for a decade
  before transitioning).

  Mann/Zinman have run this configuration for 5.5 years (since Nov 2020).
  Operationally, the company has executed exceptionally well during
  this period: revenue grew from $161M (2020) to $1,232M (2025) -- a
  7.6x in 5 years. There are no public signs of co-CEO friction.

  Risk: co-CEO fatigue. If one of them steps down in 2026-2028, the
  market would re-rate (likely down) on uncertainty. Mitigation: both
  founders are still young (Mann 47, Zinman 42) and have plenty of
  runway.

  6.2  FOUNDER OWNERSHIP AND ALIGNMENT

  As of the FY2025 20-F, founder/insider ownership is approximately:
    - Roy Mann:        ~5.5% directly + founder share veto rights
    - Eran Zinman:     ~3.5%
    - Insight Partners (board chair Jeff Horing): ~28% (largest holder)
    - Other insiders:  ~3%

  Total insider + Insight: ~40% ownership. This is high alignment.

  However, Insight Partners is a private equity firm with a finite-fund
  structure. At some point Insight will distribute or sell. This is a
  technical overhang.

  6.3  THE FOUNDER SHARE VETO

  Roy Mann's founder share grants veto rights on certain corporate
  matters (sale of company, dilutive issuances, board composition).
  This is somewhat unusual for a Nasdaq-listed FPI but not unique
  among Israeli founder-led SaaS.

  Implications for shareholders:
    - Pro: prevents value-destroying takeover at low prices (Mann
      can block a $80 takeover even if board / Insight want to sell)
    - Pro: reduces short-term-ist pressure
    - Con: blocks a value-creating takeover at fair prices (e.g. if
      Microsoft offered $200, Mann could block)
    - Con: founder-CEO with veto rights is structurally unaccountable

  In practice, founder shares of this type are rarely exercised
  controversially. The signal is that Mann does not see this as a
  short-term play -- he expects to be running this company for
  another 10-15 years. That's mostly aligning with long-term holders.

  6.4  CFO QUALITY (vs. our other top candidate's red flag)

  Eliran Glazer has been CFO since March 2021 -- 5+ years through
  the entire public company history. Prior CFO experience at
  Lightricks, NEX Markets, CME Group. CPA. No turnover risk.

  This is a meaningful structural advantage vs. TTD (where two CFO
  changes in 30 months are a primary thesis-break risk). For a

  Long-term investors look for "free options" -- products or initiatives

  vibe ARR: $1M+ as of Q4 2025 (10 weeks post-launch -- fastest in
  company history).

  probability of reaching $100M ARR by 2028 (40% probability of $50M

  the new "monday work management" category.

  Value of this option: in the base case, AI products contribute ~$200M
  by 2028 (4% of revenue). In the bull case, $500M+ (8-9% of revenue).
  Both add 5-12% to fair value vs. pure work-OS continuation.

  7.2  INTERNATIONAL EXPANSION

  monday.com is already 50% non-US revenue, but international SaaS
  penetration in EMEA/APAC is 5-7 years behind US. Significant runway.

  If international revenue grows 25% CAGR for 5 years vs. US at 12%
  CAGR, total mix shifts to 60% non-US by 2030. This drives ~200bps
  of incremental annual top-line growth above the bear-case curve.

  7.3  VERTICALIZATION

  monday.com has nascent industry templates / packaged solutions for
  construction, marketing agencies, real estate. These are early but
  align with the high-growth SMB-mid market combo.

  Each vertical productized solution that reaches >$50M ARR represents
  a ~3-5% boost to total revenue. There are 5-7 plausible verticals.

  7.4  PLATFORMIZATION / APP MARKETPLACE

  280 third-party apps in the marketplace today; revenue contribution
  from app marketplace is undisclosed but "small." Atlassian and
  Salesforce both have material marketplace revenue (~$500M+ at scale).
  monday.com is 5-7 years behind but the architecture supports it.

  7.5  LEVERAGED BUYBACK FLYWHEEL

  At $65 stock price, $735M of remaining authorization can retire
  21% of float. If executed and the stock re-rates 50% upon Q1
  confirmation, the remaining 79% of holders get a substantial
  return purely from share count compression. This is a real
  optionality vector that's worth ~10-15% of fair value alone.

  7.6  ACQUISITION TARGET OPTIONALITY (low probability)

  Founder share veto blocks unsolicited takeovers, but if Mann/Zinman
  decide to sell, monday.com would be a logical acquisition for
  Salesforce, Microsoft, ServiceNow, or Atlassian. Replacement-cost
  valuation in a strategic sale would likely be 6-8x trailing revenue
  ($7-10B EV) vs. current $1.98B EV. Not a baseline thesis but a
  3-5% probability tail risk in the holder's favor.

  TOTAL OPTIONALITY VALUE (conservative): ~15-25% of base-case fair value.
