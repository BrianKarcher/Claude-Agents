0. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — WHAT THIS DEEP DIVE FOUND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The initiation framed PUBM as a cheap, net-cash, FCF-positive, narrow-moat,
founder-led independent SSP with two free options (AgenticOS + Google remedy).
That framing survives the deep dive, but with three material refinements that
TIGHTEN the discipline rather than loosen it:

(1) THE CHEAPNESS IS REAL ONLY ON THE RIGHT METRIC, AND THINNER THAN IT LOOKS.
    PUBM screens at ~6x EV/adj-EBITDA and ~8x EV/reported-FCF — trough-decile.
    But the honest owner-earnings number is SBC-ADJUSTED FCF, and on that basis
    FY2025 was only ~$7.9M ($46.3M reported FCF minus $38.4M of SBC), a ~2.8%
    margin. EV/SBC-adj-FCF is therefore ~47x trailing, NOT 8x. The entire
    investability case rests on margin RECOVERY (EBITDA back toward high-20s/30%
    and SBC falling as a % of revenue) layered on top of revenue reacceleration.
    This is the single most important finding: PUBM is not "cheap and you wait,"
    it is "cheap ONLY IF the operating leverage and reacceleration are real."

(2) THE COMPETITIVE POSITION IS WEAKER THAN THE INITIATION IMPLIED. PUBM is the
    #3-ish independent SSP, not the #1. Magnite is roughly 5-6x larger in US SSP
    share (~25% vs ~4%) and owns the CTV channel that is the industry's growth
    engine (CTV >50% of Magnite's business vs ~39% video/omnichannel for PUBM).
    The SSP layer is consolidating (32 -> 18 players) and structurally take-rate-
    pressured. PUBM's edge is genuine but narrow: independence + owned infra +
    an aggressive (unproven) agentic-AI bet. The moat verdict moves to NARROW /
    LOW-TO-MODERATE confidence.

(3) THE GOOGLE-REMEDY OPTION IS LIVE BUT LIKELY BEHAVIORAL, NOT STRUCTURAL.
    Judge Brinkema (E.D. Va.) found Google liable (April ruling) and the remedy
    phase closing arguments are done; a ruling is expected in 2026. But she has
    signaled skepticism of a forced AdX divestiture (no clean buyer, appeal
    timing) and appears more inclined toward BEHAVIORAL remedies (auction
    interoperability, open-sourcing auction logic). Behavioral remedies still
    help independents like PUBM — they lower Google's structural auction
    advantage — but the "each 1% share = $50-75M at 80% margin / double our
    share" math is a multi-year, probabilistic call option, not a near-term
    earnings event. Plus PUBM is in active LITIGATION with Google (a contained
    but real cost). Treat the remedy as upside optionality, weight it modestly,
    and never put it in the base case.

NET: The asymmetry is attractive but NARROWER and MORE EXECUTION-DEPENDENT than
the initiation's framing. The bear case is genuinely floored by net cash
(~$3/share) plus a still-FCF-generative core, but the base case requires a
real H2-2026 reacceleration AND a multi-year margin recovery that the SBC-
adjusted numbers do not yet show. This is a "show me" small cap. The right
posture: complete the DCF (Action 4) with SBC-adjusted FCF as the binding
underwriting column, reconcile the share-count discrepancy (critical — see
Sec 1.0), and demand a wide margin of safety given the narrow moat and the
fact that the thesis is contingent on Q3 2026 actually returning to reported
growth. Rating stays WATCH / ACCUMULATE ON CONFIRMATION.

Probability weights carried into the DCF: BEAR 30% / BASE 50% / BULL 20%.
(Bear weight raised from the initiation's implied ~25% on the weaker-than-
expected competitive read and the SBC-adjusted FCF reality.)

1.0 SHARE-COUNT RECONCILIATION — A CRITICAL DATA GAP TO RESOLVE BEFORE THE DCF
The 10-K extract lists "Class A shares outstanding ~93,903,451." This does NOT
reconcile with the rest of the financials:

    (net loss $(12.5)M / 47.1M = $(0.27) GAAP EPS — checks out).
  - Market cap ~$517M at ~$11.13 implies ~46.4M shares.
  - Treasury stock $(202.3M); ~13.5M shares repurchased for ~$190M cumulatively.
The market-cap-relevant, EPS-relevant, and valuation-relevant share count is
therefore ~47-48M, NOT ~94M. The 93.9M figure is most likely a
gross/issued/authorized or pre-treasury Class A figure (or an extract mislabel),
not net shares outstanding for per-share value.

==> ACTION FOR DCF: Use ~47-48M diluted shares as the base, and BEFORE finalizing
    per-share IV, reconcile against the 10-K cover page / share-count footnote
    in the source filing. Share count drives per-share IV one-for-one; a 2x error
    here would halve the IV. This is the #1 data gap. Flag it; do not guess past it.

1.1 THE NET-REVENUE MODEL AND TAKE RATE
PUBM reports revenue NET of publisher payments and SPO rebates. That is why AR
($337M) and AP ($343M) dwarf reported revenue ($283M) — it collects gross from
buyers and remits to publishers, keeping only its take. Implications:
  - Reported "revenue" IS effectively net take. The take rate on gross spend is
    not separately disclosed cleanly, but the structural driver to watch is take
    rate per impression x impression volume.
  - Impression volume is GROWING FAST (FY2025 quarterly 74.7T->97.6T; Q1 2026
    94.2T, +26% YoY; >1T/day). Yet net revenue FELL -3% in FY2025 and -2% in Q1.
  - The arithmetic: revenue per impression is DECLINING faster than volume is
    growing. Cost per impression fell ~20% YoY (good for margins) but net take
    per impression also fell (legacy-DSP rerouting + CPM/mix). This is the
    take-rate-compression risk made concrete. The bull case needs Activate and
    AgenticOS (higher-value, full-dollar-in-ecosystem transactions) to lift
    revenue-per-impression even as commoditized open-auction take compresses.

1.2 GROSS MARGIN — OWNED-INFRA FIXED-COST LEVERAGE (DOUBLE-EDGED)
  - FY2025 gross margin ~63.6% (cost of rev $103.1M / rev $282.9M).
  - Q1 2026 gross margin 58.3% (cost of rev $26.1M / rev $62.6M).
  - The gap is seasonality: PUBM OWNS its infrastructure (no public cloud), so
    cost of revenue is largely FIXED. In seasonally weak Q1 (lowest revenue
    quarter), fixed infra costs compress gross margin; in seasonally strong Q4,
    they expand it. This is the operating-leverage engine in BOTH directions:
    revenue reacceleration drops to the gross line at high incremental margin;
    revenue stagnation crushes it. Owned infra is a moat ONLY at scale and ONLY
    if volume keeps growing into the fixed base.
  - Long-term gross-margin watch line: structural break below ~58% would signal
    take-rate compression overwhelming unit-cost gains (a thesis-break trigger).

1.3 THE EBITDA COLLAPSE AND THE SBC PROBLEM (the crux)
  Adjusted EBITDA margin: FY2023 28% -> FY2024 31.7% -> FY2025 21.8% -> Q1'26 4%.
  This is a ~10pt margin collapse in one year on a -3% revenue line. Drivers:
  S&M up (+$7.6M FY25), G&A up (litigation), revenue down, fixed infra base.
  SBC is the silent killer in the cash-flow translation:
    +-----------------------------+---------+---------+
    | Owner-earnings bridge (FY25)| $M      | % rev   |
    +-----------------------------+---------+---------+
    | OCF                         | 81.1    | 28.7%   |
    | (-) PP&E capex              | (14.3)  |         |
    | (-) Capitalized software    | (20.5)  |         |
    | = Reported FCF              | 46.3    | 16.4%   |
    | (-) SBC (real cost)         | (38.4)  | 13.6%   |
    | = SBC-ADJUSTED FCF          | ~7.9    | ~2.8%   |
    +-----------------------------+---------+---------+
  The reported 16% FCF margin is flattered by (a) $38M of SBC added back in OCF
  and (b) ~$20M of R&D expensed via the cash flow as capitalized software (a
  real cash cost of the business). On an SBC-charged basis the company earns
  ~$8M of true owner cash on $283M revenue. At EV ~$372M that is ~47x — NOT
  cheap. The DCF MUST underwrite on this number and model its recovery, not on
  the headline FCF.

1.4 WHAT WOULD NORMALIZED MARGINS LOOK LIKE?
  The bull thesis is that 2025 was a trough (legacy-DSP drag + investment year)
  and that the owned-infra fixed base + AI-driven internal productivity
  (headcount down YoY) restores EBITDA to the high-20s/30% as revenue
  reaccelerates. If revenue returns to ~$340-380M by 2028-2029 at a 28-30%
  EBITDA margin, EBITDA of ~$95-115M and SBC normalizing toward ~9-10% of
  revenue would put SBC-adj FCF margin around 12-15% (~$45-55M). THAT is the
  number that makes today's EV ~$372M look cheap (~7-8x forward SBC-adj FCF).
  But it requires BOTH the top-line reaccel AND the margin recovery — two things
  that must compound, neither yet proven in the reported numbers.

2. THE "LEGACY DSP" HEADWIND — DECOMPOSED (Marching Order #1)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This is the linchpin of the whole "trough vs. structural decline" debate.

What it is: One (then a second) large DSP buyer changed its bidding methodology /
platform behavior — first in H2 2024, then again in H2 2025 — rerouting spend
away from PUBM's path. The two largest DSP relationships are GOOGLE and THE TRADE
DESK; both contracts auto-renew and are terminable for convenience on 30 days'
notice. Management labels the affected flow "legacy DSP."

Sizing it (triangulation from disclosures):
  - FY2025 reported -3% (-$8.3M) but that nets ~$14.2M of LOST 2024 political
    spend (no 2026 analog) against underlying growth. So core ex-political was
    roughly flat-to-slightly-up reported, with the legacy-DSP drag the main
    suppressor.
  - Q1 2026: reported -2%, but ex-legacy-DSP +13% (the ex-DSP base is ~83% of
    revenue). That implies the legacy-DSP-affected ~17% of revenue declined
    enough to drag a +13% underlying into a -2% reported — roughly a ~50-60%+
    YoY decline in the affected cohort. That cohort is shrinking as a share of
    revenue precisely because it is declining, which is why the drag self-limits.
  - Lap timing: management guides the legacy DSP to lap by "mid-Q3 2026," after
    which reported growth should re-converge toward the ~13%+ underlying rate,
    accelerating to "double-digit growth in H2 2026 with margin expansion."

Is the lap credible? Partly mechanical, partly faith:
  - MECHANICAL: a cohort that has shrunk to a small share, declining against an
    easier comp, mathematically stops dragging once it laps. This part is
    high-confidence IF no NEW DSP reroutes spend.
  - FAITH: the underlying +13% must persist AND no third DSP repeats the move.
    The 30-day-termination concentration means this risk never fully goes away.
    Mitigant: mid-market DSP spend +20% YoY, 50+ DSPs added in the past year,
    Activate direct-buy >3x — genuine diversification away from the top-2 DSP
    dependence. But the top-2 (Google, TTD) still matter enormously.

VERDICT: The "trough" framing is more-likely-than-not correct (base case 50%),
but the structural-decline tail is real enough to carry a 30% bear weight. The
Q3 2026 print (return to reported growth) is THE single cleanest thesis
confirmation/refutation event. No buy should pre-empt it without a wide MoS.

3. TAM, AGENTICOS, AND THE AI DISINTERMEDIATION TAIL (Marching Order #3)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TAM shape:
  - Global programmatic advertising continues to grow (high-single/low-double
    digits), with CTV and commerce/retail media the fastest segments. Commerce
    media TAM cited ~$18B. CTV is the structural growth channel — and is exactly
    where PUBM is WEAKEST relative to Magnite (see Sec 4).
  - PUBM's serviceable opportunity is the independent (non-walled-garden,
    non-Google-owned-exchange) open-internet sell side. That pool is real but
    structurally squeezed between Google's owned exchange and the walled gardens
    (Amazon, Meta, etc.).

AgenticOS (the bull's growth + margin lever):
  - Launched Jan 2026 (CES). 20+ operational agents; >1,000 AI-powered deals;
    30+ live autonomous end-to-end campaigns across US/France/Netherlands/
    Australia/India. Claims: 80-90% campaign-setup time savings; with Activate,
    30-40% CPM improvement + 40% more working media; "every advertiser that ran
    an agentic campaign returned to run more."
  - Emerging revenue (Activate, Commerce/Convert, Connect, AI Solutions) is
    +80% YoY and ~14% of revenue. If it sustains 50%+ growth, it becomes ~25%+
    of revenue within 2-3 years and re-rates the whole company's growth profile.
  - Monetization model is still forming: Activate rev-share today; subscription
    being explored. This is the key uncertainty — agentic adoption is real but
    "those investments haven't paid off yet in the immediate term" (industry
    read). PUBM is the MOST aggressive SSP on agentic AI; that is both the bull
    differentiator and a risk if the payoff slips.

The AI disintermediation tail (the bear's existential risk):
  - If LLM platforms (OpenAI's stated ~$100B ad ambition, agentic buyers) route
    ad spend directly to supply and compress or bypass the SSP take, the open-
    internet SSP layer is structurally threatened. PUBM's counter is to BE the
    agentic execution layer (MCP integration with AdRoll; agents that external
    buyers can plug into; owned GPU/Triton inference for low-latency execution).
  - My read: agentic AI is more likely to RESHAPE the SSP than ERASE it in the
    5-10yr window — someone must still run the real-time supply auction, enforce
    publisher controls, and optimize yield. But the take rate on that function
    can compress. So the tail is "margin erosion," more than "zero." Weight it
    inside the bear case (take-rate compression), with a small disaster sub-tail.

4. COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS OVER 5-10 YEARS (Marching Order #5)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The SSP landscape (qualitative web context, 2026):
  - GOOGLE (AdX / Google Ad Manager): ~60% ad-tech share; the gorilla on both
    sides of the auction; the antitrust defendant (Sec 6).
  - MAGNITE: the largest INDEPENDENT SSP; ~25% US SSP share; CTV leader (CTV
    >50% of its business; 15-20% global programmatic CTV share; 1,500+ premium
    publishers). Notably framing AI as an EFFICIENCY play, not a bet-the-company
    pivot, while "just growing" — a pointed contrast to PUBM.
  - PUBM: ~4% SSP share; omnichannel video (incl. CTV) ~39% of FY2025 revenue;
    the most aggressive agentic-AI strategy among SSPs.
  - INDEX EXCHANGE, OPENX, SHARETHROUGH, regional SSPs: the consolidating tail.
    SSP count fell 32 (2023) -> 18 (2026).

What this means for PUBM over 5-10 years:
  - PUBM is a SUB-SCALE #3 in a consolidating, take-rate-pressured layer where
    the #2 (Magnite) is ~5-6x larger and owns the growth channel (CTV). That is
    a materially weaker competitive position than the initiation conveyed.
  - PUBM's credible edges: (a) genuine independence/neutrality vs Google's
    conflicted dual-side position; (b) owned infrastructure = structural unit-
    cost advantage at scale (cost/impression -20% YoY) and a faster AI loop;
    (c) the agentic-AI bet, IF it pays off, could leapfrog the efficiency-only
    incumbents. The "no competitor has all five" (supply scale + Activate +
    AgenticOS + owned GPU + Connect data) claim is plausible but unproven as a
    durable moat.
  - The consolidation trend cuts BOTH ways: it can squeeze a sub-scale player,
    OR it can make PUBM a beneficiary/consolidator (it has $145M net cash and
    made a $3M equity investment in Q1 — tuck-in optionality). Founder control +
    net cash also make it a plausible acquisition target at a depressed price
    (the dual-class structure protects against a low-ball takeout, which is
    shareholder-protective here).
  - MOAT VERDICT (refined): NARROW, LOW-TO-MODERATE confidence (down a notch
    from the initiation's NARROW/MODERATE). The investability rests on price +
    balance sheet + optionality, NOT on an impregnable moat. Underwrite
    accordingly: wide margin of safety, modest terminal growth, conservative
    terminal multiple.

5. THE GOOGLE AD-TECH REMEDY — SCENARIO TREE (Marching Order #4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Status (qualitative web context, June 2026): Judge Leonie Brinkema (E.D. Va.)
ruled Google liable for ad-tech monopolization (April liability ruling). The
remedy-phase closing arguments are complete; a remedies ruling is expected in
2026. The DOJ pushed for STRUCTURAL relief — divest AdX, and/or phase out/divest

SKEPTICISM of a forced AdX divestiture (no obvious clean buyer; appeal-timing
concerns) and appeared more receptive to BEHAVIORAL remedies and to weighing how
fast structural vs. behavioral relief could take effect. Google will very likely
APPEAL any structural remedy, pushing real-world effect years out.

Scenario tree for PUBM impact (probability-weighted, qualitative):
  +------+-------------------------------+--------+--------------------------------+
  | Tag  | Outcome                       | Prob   | PUBM impact                    |
  +------+-------------------------------+--------+--------------------------------+
  | R1   | Behavioral remedy (auction    | ~50%   | Mildly positive over 2-4 yrs:  |
  |      | interop / open auction logic) |        | lowers Google's structural edge|
  |      |                               |        | independents gain share slowly |
  | R2   | Structural (AdX or DFP        | ~20%   | Materially positive but DELAYED|
  |      | divest/phase-out) upheld      |        | by appeals (3-5 yrs); reshapes |
  |      |                               |        | the whole open exchange        |
  | R3   | Narrow/weak remedy or stay    | ~20%   | Negligible near-term; status   |
  |      | pending appeal                |        | quo persists                   |
  | R4   | Remedy + Google appeal wins/  | ~10%   | Negligible to slightly negative|
  |      | dilutes on appeal             |        | (litigation cost lingers)      |
  +------+-------------------------------+--------+--------------------------------+
Management's "each 1% of ~$X share captured = $50-75M revenue at ~80% margin /
can more than double our ~4% share" is directionally enormous (a 4pt share gain
= ~$200-300M of near-80%-margin revenue, i.e. potentially DOUBLING the company's
revenue at far higher margins). BUT that is a multi-year, low-base-rate call
option contingent on R1/R2 actually shifting share to a SUB-SCALE #3 (Magnite,
larger and CTV-strong, would likely capture more of any reallocated share than

==> DCF TREATMENT: KEEP THE REMEDY OUT OF THE BASE CASE. Model it ONLY in the

10. WHAT WOULD HAVE TO BE TRUE FOR THE THESIS TO BREAK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mechanically distinct break scenarios:
  (1) Q3 2026 does NOT return to reported YoY growth despite the legacy-DSP lap
      => "trough" was actually "structural decline." (PRIMARY, cleanest signal.)
  (2) A THIRD large DSP reroutes spend, or the Google/TTD relationship
      deteriorates (30-day termination risk realized again).
  (3) Gross margin breaks structurally below ~58% => take-rate compression is
      overwhelming owned-infra unit-cost gains.
  (4) Emerging/AI revenue growth decelerates below ~30% => AgenticOS optionality
      deflates; PUBM is just a sub-scale commoditized SSP.
