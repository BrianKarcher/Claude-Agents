## Shares & Ownership

- Class A shares outstanding (Dec 31, 2025): ~93,903,451
- Dual-class structure: Class A (1 vote) and Class B (10 votes); insiders control via Class B. 71 holders of record of Class B.
- Never paid a dividend; intends to retain earnings.

## Capital Return

- Repurchase program: $75M (Feb 2023) + $100M (Feb 2024) + $100M (May 2025) authorized through Dec 31, 2026.
- ~$181.1M repurchased cumulatively through Dec 31, 2025.
- FY2025 buybacks: ~$46.5M.

## Revenue Drivers / Notes

- FY2025 revenue decline driven by: (a) ~$14.2M lost 2024 US political spend (no 2026 analog in the comp), (b) bidding-methodology / platform changes by one large DSP buyer in H2 2024, and (c) a second DSP buyer's platform changes in H2 2025 ("legacy DSP" headwind).
- Two largest DSP relationships: Google LLC and The Trade Desk. Both auto-renewing, terminable for convenience on 30 days notice.
- Impressions processed grew materially: FY2025 quarterly = 74.7T / 77.7T / 86.8T / 97.6T (vs. FY2024 57.9T / 60.7T / 69.8T / 74.7T). Cost per impression down ~20% YoY.
- SPO = ~55% of total platform activity in FY2025.
- ~1,980 publishers worldwide (vs. ~1,900 in 2024).
- 1,030 employees (322 US, 573 India, 135 elsewhere) — heavy India engineering base.

## Risk Factors (selected, thesis-relevant)

- High dependence on a limited number of large DSPs (Google, TTD) for impressions purchased; pricing/bidding changes materially affect revenue (validated by the legacy DSP headwind).
- Active litigation with Google LLC (flagged as a distinct risk).
- AI adoption by customers/competitors could alter competitive dynamics and reduce demand.
- Cookie/identifier deprecation and privacy regulation (GDPR, CCPA, state laws).
- Dual-class structure: insiders have substantial control.
- India operations concentration; data-center dependence.

## Guidance (from 10-K, FY2026 directional)

- Expects revenue to increase in 2026, driven by mobile, OTT/CTV, and new AI revenue streams.
- Cost of revenue expected higher in 2026 (absolute $) on data-center costs.
- T&D roughly flat; S&M and G&A up in absolute dollars (G&A up partly on litigation expense).
- Continued impact from the DSP bidding-methodology change near term.

## One-time / accounting items

- FY2024 other income included ~$4.0M from Google Privacy Sandbox initiative (non-recurring).
- FY2025 tax benefit driven by FDII deduction + federal R&D credit (9% effective rate).
- Credit facility: $110M revolver with SVB, matures Oct 2027, undrawn, 7.75% applicable rate at YE2025.
