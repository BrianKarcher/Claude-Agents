## One-time items called out in the filing

1. **Income tax benefit of $59.4M (FY2025)** — driven by a non-cash reversal of a valuation allowance on deferred tax assets (~$61.2M of the benefit). Management concluded sustained profitability made deferred tax asset realization more-likely-than-not. This is **not recurring**; FY2026 effective tax rate will likely revert to a normalized range. GAAP EPS overstated by ~$1.16/share due to this benefit.
2. **2024 charitable contribution** — $24.2M one-time charge (equity grant + cash) to monday.com Foundation, calculated as 1% of IPO gross proceeds.
3. **Foundation grant** — $17.9M (68,000 shares contributed in 2024) included in SBC.

## Risk factors flagged

- Israel-Hamas war and regional escalation
- Founder share with veto rights (governance)
- FPI status (limited disclosure obligations vs U.S. domestic issuers)
- Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) classification risk
- Concentration on monday work management (most revenue still single product, despite multi-product push)
- FX exposure (notional foreign-currency derivatives outstanding at year end)
- AI competitive risk
- Subscription business model and customer retention dynamics
- Reliance on traditional web search (SEO traffic)
- Israel taxation regime and Treaty status

## Key takeaways for research

1. **Founder-led, founder-controlled, no debt**, $1.66B net cash (~$32/share — about 47% of current $69 price).
2. **Profitable on GAAP basis** for first time in FY2025 ($118.7M net income, but inflated by ~$61M tax benefit; "clean" net income ~$58M).
3. **Non-GAAP operating margin expansion stalled** — guided to compress from 14% to 11-12% in FY2026 due to FX + investments. This is the bear's "deceleration is structural" hook.
4. **High-quality KPIs:** $500K+ customers up 74%, $100K+ up 45%; multi-product adoption rising. Upmarket move is real.
5. **NDR softened** to 110% in Q4 2025 from 112% (anniversary of pricing actions); large-customer NDR healthier at 116%.
6. **SBC at 14.4% of revenue** — high, but trending down (15.1% FY24).
7. **Aggressive buyback program** ($735M remaining) at depressed prices is a strong shareholder-return signal.
8. **Diluted share count growing only ~1.3% YoY** — buybacks are starting to offset SBC dilution.
