## Top-of-Funnel Commentary

> Zinman: "We have nothing new to report with paid search. **Overall, the top of funnel environment remains soft, but it's pretty much in line with our expectations** that we gave in the beginning of the year. I would say that on top of that, ACV of new lands is increasing across touch and no touch. So we've seen high-quality leads coming into the platform."

## AI Work Platform & Pricing Model

> Zinman: "Not a feature release or a rebrand. We have **rearchitected the core of our platform** around single belief that work should be orchestrated between humans and AI agents at scale from a single system of records."

> Zinman: "We recently introduced a **new seats credit pricing structure for new customers**, moving to consumption-based pricing that aligns what customers pay with the value AI actually delivers."

> Zinman on existing customers: "It's going to be gradual." (Opt-in, incentivized)

> Mann to follow up: "It's not a migration. It's like a click the switch kind of thing. There's nothing they really need to do other than agree to it."

> Glazer on impact: "We didn't assume any significant impact or any impact on the numbers" (for FY26 from new pricing).

## AI Contribution to ARR — Critical Datapoint

> Mann: "Approximately 3% of our net new ARR in Q1 was driven by AI..."

> Glazer: "10% of the net new ARR approximately is coming from [AI products]..."

> Zinman clarification: "When we say AI contribution, we mean direct contribution, not contribution made possible by AI. **Currently, it doesn't include an agent's product because it was just released about a week ago**. Currently, what drives the AI revenue is our existing offering, including AI blocks, sidekick, and so on. Obviously, with agents, we have expectations for this going forward, but it's still early days."

> **Interpretation:** The 10% net-new ARR figure (Eliran's number) is the more inclusive number; the 3% (Roy's) appears to be a narrower definition. Either way, **agents are not yet contributing — material upside lever for back half of 2026 and 2027**.

## monday DB 3.0

> Zinman: "Monday DB 3.0, delivering 100x increase in scale from 100,000 items per board to over 10 million with high performance low latency execution designed to accelerate AI adoption."

## OneAI Acquisition

> Zinman: "Bringing native voice capabilities directly into the AI Work Platform... We're very excited for the Wana [OneAI] acquisition. It's a very strong team with a lot of special knowledge about those agents."
> "We plan to integrate that deeply into our AI work platform also into our CRM. In terms of the commercial side, we're still working through the precise package in pricing. But definitely, it's going to be part of our AI credit consumption model."

## Internal AI Productivity Gains

> Mann: "Since 2025, AI has driven a **32% increase in our output per developer and a 38% reduction in product time to market**. AI gives our engineers the bandwidth to be more rigorous about the architecture, edge cases and long-term maintainability."
> "We believe this is an early but meaningful signal of what AI native engineering looks like in practice."

## Capital Allocation

> Glazer: "We ended the quarter with $1.21 billion in cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities compared to $1.67 billion at the end of Q4 2025, **reflecting $553 million of shares repurchase executed during the quarter**. As of the end of Q1, approximately $182 million remained available under our existing share repurchase authorization program."
> "We now estimate that the accelerated share buyback executed during Q1 will reduce full year 2026 adjusted free cash flow by approximately $20 million."

## Gross Margin Trajectory

> Glazer: "When we were in the Investor Day, we said that we expect gross margin to be mid-80s. We were used to 90%, but because of the computing cost of AI, we said that we are expecting for the short term or for the foreseen future to have some impact. Overall, again, we're not seeing yet a significant impact on our gross margin. But we believe there is going to be **additional cost regarding computing costs related to AI**."

## Headcount & Productivity

> Glazer: "Total employee head count was 3,211 an increase of 56 employees since Q4 '25. **For the remainder of fiscal year 2026, we expect head count to stay largely flat**, reflecting the productivity gains AI is already delivering across our organization."

## Multi-Product Attach

> Glazer: "34% of our 50,000 customers cohort has adopted more than one product. It was 29% in Q4. So we're seeing a lot of positive signs." (+500bps QoQ — fastest progression in MNDY history per deep dive context.)

## Pipeline Strength

> Casey George (CRO): "Pipeline is very strong. As a matter of fact, **our March was 1 of the strongest months we've ever had**." (Mirror of TTD's commentary same week.)

## Service / CRM Cross-Sell

> Zinman: "New products account for over 11% of our ARR. We see significant opportunity to accelerate cross-sell penetration, especially in mid-market segments. CRM, as we said, we surpassed $100 million. We continue to grow very nicely, mostly in the SMB segment."
> "In regards to service, we still see 70% of our ARR coming from mid-market and enterprise. So the ACV is the highest across all products. Most of the growth with service is driven by seat expansion and cross-sell with customer support workflows."

## Claude / AI Competitive Dynamics (Mark Murphy / JPM question)

> Mann: "We see a lot of customers having purchased Claude. And what our vision is, is having agents and people work together on the same platform, and that also includes external agents... So this is the future we see, and that's like perfectly aligned, and it's great to see adoption of AI."
>
> Zinman: "There's a big difference between people using Claude or equivalent products on their own and using that around work with other people in cooperation... I think where we shine is exactly how customers want to adopt it in a way that's kind of integrating their work, working with other people and native agents built within a work platform."

> **Interpretation:** Coexistence framing, not displacement. Deep dive flagged AI-native upstarts as a 35% downside scenario for SMB ceiling but not enterprise — Q1 commentary supports the enterprise resilience thesis.

## H2 2026 Guide Philosophy

> Glazer on Q2 guide implying H2 moderation: "On the cost side, we are investing in our product and GTM capabilities. **The potential increase of expenses from AI, as I mentioned earlier, compute. We are expecting this to be increasing throughout the year**. And there is an inherent uncertainty on how that revenue ramps when we are thinking about throughout the rest of the year. So when we take all of this together into account, our guidance does imply some moderation in H2."

## Thesis-Relevant Read (vs. Deep Dive / DCF Buy Gates)

### Confirmation of deep-dive bear/bull axis

The deep dive (sec 4) framed the FY26 deceleration as ~35% cyclical / ~35% structural / ~30% transitory comps + investment lag. The Q1 print and call **clarify the breakdown**:
- **Cyclical (~35%):** Top-of-funnel SMB softness — confirmed by Zinman ("top of funnel environment remains soft"). FX -190bps adds to this.
- **Structural-ish (~35%):** The 2024 pricing-action +12% NDR contribution is lapping out at end of Q2 — confirmed. This was always a one-time tailwind that will appear as deceleration. Not a true structural moat issue.
- **Transitory comps + investment lag (~30%):** 2025 was a GTM-rebuild year (new CRO/CMO/CCO); 2026 H1 still digesting. AI compute cost ramp is the additional 2026 lever — confirmed.

The deep dive's call that the structural component is less acute than market pricing is **further supported** by the Q1 data: gross retention at all-time highs, $500K customers +74%, multi-product attach +500bps QoQ.

Voice agents (OneAI) and monday DB 3.0 add architecture moats.
