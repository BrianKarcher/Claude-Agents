### Red flags vs. thesis

1. **Revenue growth decelerated to +12% from +18% FY25 and +25% Q1'25.** Q2 guide of "at least $750M" against $694M comparable implies +~8% YoY — further deceleration. This is sharper than the deep dive's base-case assumption.
2. **Adj EBITDA margin -400bps YoY** to 30% in Q1, despite full-year +40% guide. Implies meaningful margin expansion in H2 — execution-dependent.
3. **CFO still interim.** No permanent hire announced 7 months into transition (since fall 2024 watch period; user's deep-dive watchpoint was permanent CFO by July 2026 — still ~2 months of runway).
4. **Macro/geopolitical/tariff commentary** is heavier than initiation captured. Green explicitly called out CPG, Auto, Home & Garden, Food & Drink as weak. This is the cyclical-vs-structural deceleration question from the deep dive.

### Green flags vs. thesis

1. **Revenue beat of ~$11M vs. guide of $678M** (+1.6% beat). Pricing power and demand resilient.
2. **JBP signings +55% YoY** with March a record month — leading indicator of FY26 revenue.
3. **International outpacing US** — geographic diversification thesis playing out.
4. **Audio +fastest channel growth** + **CTV low-50s%** — premium-inventory thesis intact.
5. **Net cash grew to $1.41B** despite $164M of buybacks — cash generation engine durable.
6. **OpenAds publisher wave** (AccuWeather, Hearst, BuzzFeed, Guardian, Newsweek, People, Ziff Davis) — supply-side ecosystem widening.
7. **Pharma win against Amazon** (+114% spend) — directly rebuts the Amazon DSP thesis.
8. **LinkedIn picked TTD as first DSP partner for B2B CTV** — Microsoft-stack endorsement; partial offset to walled-garden worry.
