**The 2026 guide is the catalyst for the stock's collapse:**
- Revenue decelerating from 27% to 18-19%
- Non-GAAP operating margin compressing from 14% to 11-12%
- FCF down ~10-15% in absolute dollars (first FCF decline in company history post-IPO)
- FX is blamed for ~100-200bps; the rest is investments and macro mix

This is the central question for the bull/bear thesis: **is this guidance prudence + temporary FX, or is it the start of structural deceleration?**

## Key takeaways for research

1. **Q4 revenue +25% beats decelerating expectations**; full-year +27% is solid.
2. **Q1 2026 guide of +20% growth is reasonable**, suggesting deceleration is gradual not cliff.
3. **FY2026 FCF margin guide of 19-20% is the bear's strongest argument** — 600-700bps compression vs FY2025's 26%.
4. **Buybacks now offsetting SBC dilution** (diluted share count flat YoY in Q4) — material capital allocation improvement.
5. **Enterprise traction is the bull case**: $500K+ customers +74%, $100K+ +45%, RPO +37% all far outpacing revenue growth, suggesting backlog/lock-in is building.
6. **Co-CEO + new CRO + new CMO + new CCO** — major GTM team rebuild during 2025; expect 12-18 months for impact to land.
7. **Tax benefit of $61M cosmetically inflates GAAP earnings** — investors should adjust 2025 P/E off normalized $58M GAAP net income (~$1.10 EPS), giving "clean" P/E of ~63x. Non-GAAP P/E of ~16x is more useful framing.
