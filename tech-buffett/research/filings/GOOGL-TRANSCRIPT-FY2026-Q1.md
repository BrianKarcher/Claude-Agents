## 1. Cloud Trajectory (Most Material Section)

### Growth metrics from call
- Cloud Q1 revenue: **$20.0B**, +63% YoY (vs. $12.26B Q1'25), first-ever $20B quarter
- **GCP "grew at a rate much higher than Cloud's overall revenue growth rate"** (>63%)
- Revenue from products built on Gen AI models grew **~800% YoY** — Pichai
- **New customer acquisition doubled** vs. same period last year
- **$100M-$1B deals doubled** YoY; multiple billion-dollar+ deals signed
- Existing customers outpaced initial commitments by **45%**, accelerating from prior quarter
- 330 Cloud customers each processed >1T tokens; 35 reached 10T tokens
- BigQuery + Gemini workflows grew **>30x YoY**
- Gemini Enterprise paid MAU +40% QoQ
- Partner-driven seats: **9x YoY** growth

### Demand signal (critical Pichai quote)
> "Obviously, we are compute constrained in the near term. And as an example, our Cloud revenue would have been higher if we were able to meet the demand."

### Backlog composition
- Total Cloud RPO: $462B at end of Q1 (nearly doubled QoQ from ~$240B)
- Includes TPU hardware sale agreements (new)
- "**Majority** of backlog is typical GCP contracts"
- **"Just over 50%" expected to convert to revenue over next 24 months**

### Cloud margin commentary
- Q1 Cloud OM: **32.9%** (up from 17.8% Q1'25; +1,510 bps YoY)
- Cloud Operating Income: $6.6B, tripled YoY
- **Wiz dilution: "low single digit percentage point headwind to Cloud's operating margin for the remainder of 2026"** — Anat explicit guidance
- Implication: Cloud OM ex-Wiz running ~33-34%; reported FY26 trough ~29-31%
- Cloud margin expansion drivers per Anat:
  - Top-line operating leverage
  - "Scientific process innovation" in technical infrastructure
  - Real estate footprint optimization
  - Internal Gemini use for coding/productivity
  - Allocation of cost based on consumption (within technical infra)

### TPU hardware as a new business line
- "We will begin to deliver TPUs to a select group of customers in their own data centers in a hardware configuration to expand our addressable market opportunity"
- Customer types: AI labs, capital markets firms, HPC applications
- Revenue recognition: **small percent in 2026, vast majority in 2027**
- Quarter-to-quarter volatility expected (shipment-based)
- Pichai framed as "opportunistic" within Cloud ROIC framework
- Reflected in Cloud RPO

### TPU/silicon stack at Cloud Next
- New 8th gen TPUs (announced last week): **TPU 8t** (training) — 3x processing of Ironwood, 2x perf; **TPU 8i** (inference) — 80% better perf/$ vs prior gen
- Will be among first to offer NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72
- Continues to offer Blackwell + Hopper instances

## 2. CapEx Trajectory (Single Most Material Change to DCF)

### FY26 explicit guidance
- **New range: $180-190B** (up from $175-185B prior)
- Increase explicitly attributed to **Intersect acquisition close** (March 2026)
- Q1 capex alone: $35.7B (annualized $143B — but back half ramps materially)
- Q1 mix: ~60% servers, ~40% data centers + networking equipment

### FY27 guidance (qualitative)
- Anat: **"we expect our 2027 CapEx to significantly increase compared to 2026"**
- Doug Anmuth (JPM) directly asked for clarification; Anat declined to quantify, said "more clarity in a future earnings call"
- Driver: $462B Cloud RPO + sustained Search/Services demand + compute-constrained position

### Capex framework commentary (Pichai)
- "Robust long range planning framework"
- "ROIC framework" mentioned 4x in Q&A
- Pichai: "we are compute constrained in the near term"
- Allocation prioritization (Michael Nathanson question):
  1. GDM training compute (foundation for everything)
  2. Long-range plans for core (Search, YouTube)
  3. Cloud enterprise solutions
  4. Cloud infrastructure
  5. TPU hardware sales

### Supply chain
- Working through "complicated supply chain environment"; factoring inflation into commentary
- Diversified business + scale + supply chain partnerships called out as advantages

## 3. Search Trajectory

### Growth metrics
- Search & Other Q1: $60.4B, **+19% YoY** (vs. $50.7B Q1'25)
- "Queries at an all-time high"
- Drivers: Retail, Finance, Health verticals (in that order)
- "Each year, the queries continue to grow"

### AI commentary
- AI Overviews + AI Mode "driving overall Search growth" and growth in commercial queries
- Personal Intelligence (proprietary personalization) expanded broadly in US
- Multimodal: Search Live now in all countries supporting AI Mode
- Latency: reduced Search latency by **>35% over past 5 years**
- **"Since upgrading AI Overviews and AI Mode to Gemini 3, we have reduced the cost of core AI responses by more than 30%"** — critical for unit-economics of AI Search

### Monetization coverage (Doug Anmuth question)
- Schindler: "there is upside in that coverage number" (vs. historic ~20%)
- Driven by Gemini's ability to better understand intent on longer, complex queries
- "Gemini on intent has significantly expanded our ability to deliver Ads on longer, more complex searches that were previously really difficult to monetize"

### Ads platform progression
- Gemini deployed across all ads infrastructure
- AI Max moved out of beta with improvements
- Smart Bidding uses Gemini for granular intent matching
- Hilton EMEA case study: 1/3 more clicks for 1/5 the spend, +55% booking value
- Etsy case study: +10% search volume uplift; 15% net new queries
- ">30% of customers' Search spend now uses AI-enabled campaigns" (AI Max + Performance Max)

### Agentic commerce (UCP)
- Universal Commerce Protocol launched January 2026
- New members announced (call date): Amazon, Meta, Microsoft, Salesforce, Stripe
- Founding members: Shopify, Etsy, Target, Wayfair, Google
- Sephora, Macy's joining; Ulta Beauty already live in AI Mode + Gemini app
- "Direct Offers in AI Mode" pilot — Gap, L'Oréal, Chewy signed up
- Implication: Search becomes the launch surface for agentic commerce — early-stage but credible monetization optionality

## 13. Net DCF Impact Summary (Pre-Revision)

The transcript is **net bearish for near-term FCF** (capex guide raised meaningfully into FY26 and explicitly FY27) but **net bullish for long-term revenue trajectory and Cloud margin durability** (compute-constrained framing, Q1 Cloud OM ex-Wiz ~34%, $462B RPO).

**Primary DCF revisions required:**
1. Raise FY26-30 capex curve materially in **all three scenarios** (the $180-190B guide is real, not scenario-dependent)
2. Acknowledge buyback non-resumption as a continuing gate-fail criterion
3. Cloud OM Q1'26 supports the base case OM trajectory but does NOT clearly justify revising it up given the explicit Wiz headwind
4. TPU hardware sales add modest Cloud revenue in 2027 onward but are also lower-margin than software

**Net effect on prob-weighted IV:** Likely **slightly lower** (capex revisions outweigh Cloud margin durability) — directionally **reinforces the HOLD/WATCH rating**, not change it.
