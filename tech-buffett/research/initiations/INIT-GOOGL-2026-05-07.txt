0. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alphabet is the largest single research gap in the candidate universe. The
business compounded revenue from $307.4B (2023) to $402.8B (2025) -- a 14.5%
CAGR -- while raising operating margins from 27.4% to 32.0%. The recent Q1
2026 print, dated April 29, 2026, is the most consequential data point for
this name in years: Google Cloud revenue grew +63% YoY to $20.0B and Cloud
operating margin expanded from 17.8% to 32.9% in a single quarter, while
Cloud backlog roughly doubled QoQ to >$460B. The four-quarter narrative that
GOOGL was an AI laggard is now empirically inverted -- GOOGL is showing the
fastest cloud growth of the three hyperscalers (vs. AWS +28% and Azure
~39-40%) and more importantly the fastest cloud margin expansion. The full-
stack approach (TPUs + Gemini + Vertex + Workspace) is monetizing.

Yet the market is not euphoric. At $397/share and ~22x forward P/E, GOOGL
trades roughly in line with its 5-year average and at a discount to MSFT
(~26x), AMZN (~35x), and META (~22x). Three offsetting concerns explain the
multiple: (i) DOJ Search remedy is a final judgment as of December 2025 (on
appeal); (ii) DOJ Ad Tech remedy is pending with structural-divestiture risk
on publisher tools; (iii) capex is doubling from $91.4B in FY25 toward a
likely $140-150B+ pace in FY26, which is depressing the very FCF metric we
use to underwrite the business (SBC-adj FCF margin compressed from 14.3% in
FY24 to 12.0% in FY25, and Q1'26 SBC-adj FCF dropped to $3.4B from $13.4B in
Q1'25 -- a 75% YoY collapse driven by capex front-loading).

This is a classic Buffett setup -- a wide-moat business with multi-year
durability, trading at a reasonable price because of legible-but-bounded
risks (antitrust + capex digestion). The question for the deep dive and DCF
is not whether GOOGL is a quality business (it is, with HIGH confidence) but
rather: (a) what is the steady-state SBC-adj FCF margin once capex
normalizes, (b) what is the binding constraint from antitrust remedies on
revenue and operating profit, and (c) is the Cloud margin expansion in Q1
durable or a one-quarter optical artifact.

PRELIMINARY RATING: ACCUMULATE (pending Deep Dive + DCF confirmation).
PRELIMINARY POSITION SIZING (subject to DCF): large-cap anchor #2 alongside

1. BUSINESS DESCRIPTION
================================================================================

Alphabet Inc. is the holding company successor to Google, formed in 2015. It
operates two reporting segments plus a third "Other Bets" portfolio:

  GOOGLE SERVICES   - Search, YouTube, Android, Chrome, Gmail, Google Maps,
                      Play Store, Pixel devices, Google One, NFL Sunday
                      Ticket. Generates revenue from advertising (~86% of
                      Services), subscriptions (~14% and rising), and devices.
                      FY25 revenue: $342.7B (85% of total).

  GOOGLE CLOUD      - GCP (infrastructure, Vertex AI, Gemini Enterprise),
                      Google Workspace (Gmail/Docs/Drive/Meet for biz),
                      cybersecurity (Mandiant + Wiz). Generates revenue from
                      consumption-based fees and subscriptions.
                      FY25 revenue: $58.7B (15% of total).

  OTHER BETS        - Waymo (autonomous mobility -- now 500K rides/wk),
                      Verily, Calico, X moonshots. Operating loss $7.5B FY25.

Approximately 80% of Alphabet revenue is advertising-derived (Search +
YouTube + Google Network = $294.7B FY25 = 73% of total; plus a portion of
Google subscriptions/devices), but the trajectory is clear: Cloud is now a
$80B annualized run-rate business growing 63% YoY with 33% operating margins
-- it is becoming a meaningful second leg of the stool.

Founded:   1998 (Larry Page + Sergey Brin)
Re-orged:  2015 (Alphabet holding co.)
Founders:  No longer in operating roles; retain Class B supervoting shares.
CEO:       Sundar Pichai (CEO Alphabet & Google since Dec 2019; Google CEO
           since Aug 2015). Long-tenured operator; Stanford/Wharton; joined
           Google 2004 (product mgmt for Toolbar -> Chrome -> Android ->
           overall Google).
CFO:       Anat Ashkenazi (joined July 2024 from Eli Lilly; succeeded Ruth
           Porat who became President & CIO). New, capable, but only ~21
           months on the job at the close of Q1 2026 -- a yellow flag in the
           sense that she is still establishing capital-allocation patterns.
Listing:   NASDAQ. Class A (GOOGL, 1 vote), Class B (founders, 10 votes,
           ~836M shares), Class C (GOOG, no votes). Total 12,088M shares
           outstanding at Dec 31, 2025; 12,116M at Mar 31, 2026.

2. PRODUCTS, SEGMENTS, AND THE Q1 2026 ACCELERATION
================================================================================

2.1 GOOGLE SERVICES (FY25 $342.7B / 85% of revenue / 40.7% operating margin)

  Search & other  ($224.5B FY25, +13% YoY; $60.4B Q1'26, +19% YoY)
    - The single most valuable advertising surface in the world.
    - 19% Q1'26 growth is a re-acceleration from low-double-digits in FY24
      and ~12% in FY25. Pichai cited "queries at an all-time high" with AI
      experiences (AI Overviews + AI Mode) driving usage.
    - The bear case for years has been "ChatGPT/perplexity will erode
      Search." The Q1 print is the strongest empirical refutation we have
      seen: Search rev growth re-accelerated, not decelerated.
    - Monetization metrics in 10-K: paid clicks +6%, cost-per-click +7%
      (FY25 vs FY24). Healthy.

  YouTube ads  ($40.4B FY25, +12% YoY; $9.9B Q1'26, +11% YoY)
    - Stable mid-teens grower. Direct-response > brand drove FY25 growth.
    - Connected TV exposure indirect (via YouTube on TV apps) competes
      with TTD/AMZN DSP demand-side and with Netflix/Disney+ on ad-tier
      time spent.
    - Shorts monetization gap to long-form is closing but not closed --
      remains a multi-year tailwind.

  Google Network  ($29.8B FY25, -2% YoY; $7.0B Q1'26, -4% YoY)
    - AdSense + AdMob + Ad Manager third-party publisher network.
    - Declining segment. The DOJ Ad Tech case is most directly threatening
      to this segment (publisher tools were ruled anticompetitive April
      2025). Even pre-remedy, the segment is in secular decline as
      publishers shift inventory to first-party direct + alt-SSPs (PubMatic,
      Magnite, etc.).

  Google subscriptions, platforms, devices  ($48.0B FY25, +19% YoY; $12.4B
    Q1'26, +19% YoY)
    - YouTube TV, YouTube Music/Premium, NFL Sunday Ticket, Google One
      (which includes Gemini Advanced).
    - 350M total paid subscribers as of Q1'26 (Pichai's number).
    - This is the underrated, recurring-revenue compounder buried inside
      Google Services. ~20% growth on a $48B base = $9.6B incremental
      revenue per year, mostly margin-rich subscriptions.

  Services operating margin: 39.8% FY24 -> 40.7% FY25 -> 45.3% Q1'26.
  The Q1'26 margin print is the strongest in modern Alphabet history. To
  what degree this is sustainable vs. cyclical (FX, mix, ad-spend strength)
  is a question for the deep dive.

2.2 GOOGLE CLOUD (FY25 $58.7B / 15% of revenue / 23.7% operating margin)

  This is the segment that has changed the GOOGL thesis materially in 2026.

  FY25 revenue: $58.7B (+36% YoY). Already accelerating from ~31% growth in
  FY24. Operating income: $13.9B (+128% YoY). Operating margin 23.7% --
  still below AWS (~37%) but the gap is closing.

  Q1'26 revenue: $20.0B (+63% YoY). Operating income $6.6B (+203% YoY).
  Operating margin 32.9% -- a +1,510bps step-up YoY in a single quarter.
  This is unusual and warrants stress-testing in the deep dive (mix shift?
  one-time licensing? larger commitments cohort)? But the directional
  evidence -- combined with Pichai's $460B backlog claim (nearly double
  QoQ) -- suggests this is structural, not optical.

  Three sub-businesses inside Cloud:
    - Google Cloud Platform (GCP):  AI Infrastructure (TPU), AI Solutions
      (Vertex AI / Gemini Enterprise), core compute/storage/database. The
      growth engine. Pichai called out "enterprise AI Solutions and
      enterprise AI Infrastructure" plus core GCP services.
    - Google Workspace:  Gmail/Docs/Drive/Meet for business, with Gemini
      built in. Steady mid-teens grower.
    - Cybersecurity:  Mandiant (acquired 2022, $5.4B) plus Wiz (closing
      Q1 2026, $32B). Threat intel + cloud security posture management.

  COMPETITIVE READ-THROUGHS:
    - AWS (Q1'26 +28%, 3-yr fastest, but ~37% margin)
    - Azure (Q1'26 ~39-40%)
    - GCP (Q1'26 +63%)
  GCP is now the fastest-growing of the three at sub-scale (~$80B run-rate
  vs Azure ~$300B+ and AWS ~$120B+). The full-stack story (own TPUs +
  own foundation model Gemini + own enterprise AI tools) is the moat.

2.3 OTHER BETS (FY25 $1.5B revenue / -$7.5B operating loss)

  Waymo is the only material asset:
    - 500,000+ fully autonomous rides per week as of Q1'26 (Pichai)
    - Feb 2026 funding round of $16B (significant majority Alphabet-funded)
    - Q4'25 included $2.1B employee comp charge for Waymo (R&D line)
  Verily, Calico, X moonshots are immaterial.

  We treat Other Bets as zero-or-positive optionality for valuation purposes
  -- the operating loss is real but Waymo's option value is non-trivial.

  Customer count: millions of advertisers globally; no single customer >1%

  Note: DV360 is GOOGL's enterprise DSP, the direct competitor to TTD. We

  is in part a thesis that GOOGL DV360 is constrained from monetizing as
  aggressively due to antitrust / conflicts.

3.2 CLOUD CUSTOMERS (Google Cloud)

  Backlog: >$460B Remaining Performance Obligations as of Q1'26 (per
  Pichai). For comparison, MSFT RPO was $625B at Dec 2025. AWS does not
  publish equivalent RPO.

  Customer mix: not disclosed at granularity, but commentary calls out
  large enterprise AI infrastructure deals as the driver of the backlog
  doubling.

  Multi-year, multi-billion-dollar enterprise commitments are now the
  dominant unit of Cloud growth, similar to Azure's pattern from 2022-25.
  This is the highest-quality flavor of recurring revenue in tech.

3.3 SUBSCRIPTION CUSTOMERS (Services)

  350M paid subscriptions across YouTube TV, YouTube Music/Premium, NFL
  Sunday Ticket, and Google One. This is a true consumer-recurring-revenue
  business hidden inside an ad-tech reporting segment.

4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE & MOAT ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

4.1 SEARCH ECOSYSTEM (the legacy moat under acute LLM pressure)

  Direct competitors:
    - Microsoft Bing (with OpenAI integration)
    - Perplexity AI (private, $9B post-money)
    - OpenAI (ChatGPT browse mode, ChatGPT Atlas browser)
    - Anthropic Claude (with web search)
    - Brave Search, DuckDuckGo

  GOOGL's response: AI Overviews (now standard on most queries), AI Mode
  (full conversational search), Gemini App as standalone consumer product.
  Pichai cited 16B tokens/min via direct API (+60% QoQ) -- usage
  acceleration, not decay.

  MOAT VERDICT FOR SEARCH: WIDE, but narrowing at the edges.
    - Distribution moat (Chrome 65% browser share, default search on iOS
      via Apple TAC paying $20B+/yr, default on Android) -- attacked by
      DOJ remedies but final remedies stayed pending appeal.
    - Data moat (20+ years of query/click data) -- still unmatched but
      LLM-on-web data narrows the gap.
    - Brand moat ("just google it" is in the dictionary) -- intact.
    - Habit moat (4B+ users) -- intact and reinforced by AI experiences
      driving usage growth.

4.2 YOUTUBE (the durable second moat)

  Direct competitors: TikTok (international, attacked by app stores +
  geopolitics), Instagram Reels, Twitch (Amazon), Netflix on the long-form
  side.

  YouTube's moat:
    - Network effects between creators and viewers (creator economy via
      YouTube Partner Program)
    - 2B+ logged-in monthly users (last disclosed)
    - Cross-platform: phone, tablet, TV (#1 connected TV property in many
      markets)
    - YouTube Shorts is now competitive with TikTok/Reels on time spent
      among under-25 demos.

  MOAT VERDICT: WIDE. TikTok remains the only structural threat and is
  geopolitically constrained.

4.3 GOOGLE CLOUD (the new growth engine)

  Direct competitors: AWS, Azure. Then Oracle Cloud, Alibaba (China only),
  IBM (legacy).

  GOOGL's structural advantages:
    - TPU (Tensor Processing Units): the only major hyperscaler with a
      first-party AI accelerator at scale (Gemini training + inference on
      TPU; available to enterprise customers via Vertex). NVIDIA/CUDA is
      still dominant in the broader market but TPU is a real second source
      and a Google P&L lever.
    - Gemini foundation models (1st party, no OpenAI dependency -- the
      single biggest contrast with Microsoft).
    - Vertex AI + Gemini Enterprise as the application layer.
    - DeepMind + Google Research talent density.

  Structural disadvantages:
    - Smaller than AWS and Azure -- still ~6-7x smaller than Azure on rev.
    - Enterprise sales motion historically weaker than MSFT (which has
      40+ years of Fortune 500 relationships).
    - Trust gap: some Fortune 500s historically skeptical that Google
      treats enterprise as a strategic segment vs. an ad sideshow. The
      Q1 print and $460B backlog are pushing back on that skepticism.

  MOAT VERDICT FOR CLOUD: NARROW now, NARROWING-TO-WIDE in 5 years if
  the Q1 trajectory holds. Biggest wildcard in the deep dive.

4.4 ANDROID + PLAY STORE (the hidden distribution moat)

  3B+ Android devices globally. Play Store is a 30%-take rent extraction
  business (lower in some categories post-Epic vs. Google ruling).
  DOJ-imposed Play Store remedies are still being negotiated.

  MOAT VERDICT: WIDE on distribution (no realistic alternative mobile OS
  outside Apple). NARROWING on monetization (regulatory pressure on rake).

4.5 AGGREGATE MOAT ASSESSMENT

  Five moat sources, framework per Buffett/Munger:
    (i) INTANGIBLES (brand, search/YouTube/Android brands):  WIDE
   (ii) SWITCHING COSTS (Workspace, Cloud, Android dev tooling):  MEDIUM
        (Cloud rising; Search/YouTube low for individual users but high
         for ecosystem participants like creators and advertisers)
  (iii) NETWORK EFFECTS (Search query-data flywheel, YouTube creator-
        viewer flywheel, Android dev-user flywheel):  WIDE
   (iv) SCALE ECONOMICS (data center capex, TPU manufacturing scale,
        global ad inventory):  WIDE -- and getting wider with $140B+
        FY26 capex
    (v) COST/EFFICIENCY (vertical integration in TPU, in-house Gemini
        training, AI-driven ad targeting):  WIDE

  Overall: WIDE moat, HIGH confidence.

  Compared to MSFT (also WIDE/HIGH): the moats are different in flavor.
  MSFT's moat is enterprise switching costs + ecosystem lock-in. GOOGL's
  moat is consumer attention + network effects + scale economics. Both
  are durable. GOOGL is arguably more exposed to AI-disruption risk
  (search) but also arguably more exposed to AI-tailwind upside (TPU +
  Gemini full stack). Net: comparable quality, with higher variance.

CEO Sundar Pichai (53)
  - CEO of Alphabet & Google since Dec 2019 (Google CEO since Aug 2015).
  - Joined Google 2004; led Toolbar, Chrome, Android, Workspace before

  - Engineer-operator profile. Conservative communicator. Has steered

  - 2025 capital allocation: $45.4B in buybacks, $10B in dividends,

CFO Anat Ashkenazi
  - Joined July 2024 from Eli Lilly (where she was CFO).
  - Pharma-CFO background is unusual for Big Tech. Less obviously domain-
    fluent on technical infrastructure but well-credentialed on capital
    allocation and large-number discipline.
  - Track record at GOOGL still being established. Q1 2026 buyback pause
    ($0 vs. ~$15B Q1'25) is the single biggest signal so far -- it is
    almost certainly tied to the Wiz close + $31B notes issuance + capex
    ramp, but warrants monitoring in case it indicates a structural shift
    in capital return policy.

Insider ownership
  - Larry Page and Sergey Brin retain Class B supervoting shares (~836M
    Class B at Dec 31, 2025) -- they collectively control >50% of voting
    power despite owning ~14% of total economic shares.
  - This is a governance flag (founder veto) but historically benign.
    They have not used voting power to override management since the
    holding-company restructuring.

Compensation/SBC discipline
  - SBC was $25.0B in FY25 = 6.2% of revenue (down from 6.5% in FY24).
  - For a Big Tech peer comp: META ~9%, MSFT ~4%, AMZN ~7% of revenue.
  - GOOGL's SBC ratio is mid-pack. Trending in the right direction.

Capital allocation grade: A-
  - Strong: aggressive buybacks (16% of FY25 FCF returned via buyback),
    dividend instituted 2024 and growing, sensible large M&A (Wiz
    cybersecurity), enormous Gemini/TPU R&D returning early signs of
    payoff.
  - Watch: the Q1 buyback pause; the magnitude of FY26 capex (~$140B+)
    versus the marginal ROI thereof. If the Cloud margin expansion
    trajectory holds, capex is value-creating. If Cloud margins flatten
    at 30-33% and growth decelerates, FY26 capex risks becoming a
    classic AI-overbuild moment.

6. KEY RISKS
================================================================================

6.1 ANTITRUST / REGULATORY (probability-weighted highest impact risk)

  6.1.1 DOJ Search Case (US v. Google, search distribution):
    - Final judgment Dec 2025 imposes restrictions on distribution
      agreements (Apple TAC, Android default-search) and requires Google
      to share certain search data with competitors and offer syndication
      services.
    - Alphabet appealed Jan 2026; DOJ also appealed Feb 2026. Remedies
      stayed pending appeal.
    - WORST CASE: divestiture of Chrome -- low probability in our view
      (5-10%) but a tail risk that would materially recut the moat.
    - LIKELY CASE: behavioral remedies on default-search payments (the
      $20B+/yr Apple TAC), data-sharing requirements. Net annual EBIT
      hit estimate $5-12B (conservative). Manageable on a $129B FY25
      operating income base.

  6.1.2 DOJ Ad Tech Case (US v. Google, ad tech stack):
    - April 2025: publisher tools (DoubleClick for Publishers / Ad
      Manager) ruled anticompetitive. Advertiser tools cleared.
      DoubleClick + AdMeld acquisitions cleared.
    - Remedy phase Sept-Nov 2025; final judgment pending as of 10-K
      filing.
    - DOJ proposed remedies include structural divestiture of publisher
      tools.
    - WORST CASE: forced divestiture of Google Ad Manager. Direct
      revenue at risk: Google Network ($29.8B FY25, declining). EBIT
      impact more contained because Network has lower margins.
    - LIKELY CASE: behavioral remedies + partial divestiture of
      publisher-side ad server. EBIT hit $1-3B/yr. Optically negative
      but manageable.

  6.1.3 EC fines (cumulative -- already in numbers):
    - Recent: $3.5B EC fine accrued Q3 2025 (already in FY25 G&A).
    - Historical: €2.4B (2017 Shopping), €4.3B (2018 Android), €1.5B
      (2019 AdSense). All on appeal through European courts.
    - Run-rate EC fine exposure: low single-digit billions annually
      until DMA/DSA enforcement settles. Manageable.

  6.1.4 Texas AG Ad Tech case + private follow-on litigation:
    - Trial date follows DOJ remedies decision.
    - Will likely produce additional behavioral/monetary remedies.
    - Materiality: low single-digit billions in damages plus ongoing
      compliance cost.

  AGGREGATE ANTITRUST IMPACT (our preliminary estimate, to be refined in
  deep dive): $6-15B annual EBIT impairment by 2028-2030 (vs. ~$170B+
  base-case operating income), or ~3-9% EBIT haircut. Stock multiple
  impact: a 1-2x P/E compression effect that may already be in the
  current multiple.

6.2 AI DISRUPTION / SEARCH SUBSTITUTION

  Bear thesis: ChatGPT/Perplexity/Claude erode Search query share. Long-
  term threat: ad-supported business model dilutes as users consume
  fewer-but-higher-quality LLM answers without monetizable clicks.

  Counter-evidence (Q1 2026 print):
    - Search rev growth +19% Q1'26 (re-acceleration, not deceleration)
    - Pichai cited "queries at all-time high"
    - AI Overviews + AI Mode driving usage rather than substituting it
    - 350M paid subscribers (incl. Google One/Gemini Advanced)

  Our view: the bear thesis is real over 5-10 years but the empirical
  evidence in 2026 is that GOOGL is winning the response cycle. We will
  stress-test this in the deep dive with TAM scenarios.

6.3 CAPEX RETURN UNCERTAINTY (the FCF compression risk)

  - FY24 capex: $52.5B
  - FY25 capex: $91.4B (+74% YoY)
  - Q1'26 capex: $35.7B (annualized $143B; 10-K MD&A confirms FY26 will
    be "significantly increased" vs FY25)
  - FY26 estimate: $140-160B range

  Implications:
    - SBC-adj FCF margin already compressed from 14.3% (FY24) to 12.0%
      (FY25). In Q1'26, SBC-adj FCF dropped to $3.4B (3.1% margin) vs.
      $13.4B (14.9%) in Q1'25.
    - On a TTM basis, FCF is $64.4B vs. $73.3B FY25 -- already declining.
    - If capex stays at $140B+ for 3+ years (i.e., a longer supercycle
      than our base case), then DCF-implied intrinsic value is materially
      lower.

  This is the central DCF question -- conviction on capex normalization
  curve is what determines the IV.

6.4 OPENAI / FRONTIER MODEL DISTILLATION

  Less central than for MSFT, but real: if OpenAI/Anthropic continue to
  build moats on multimodal frontier models, GOOGL's Gemini differentiation
  must keep pace. Track record: Gemini 2.5 Pro and Gemini Ultra are at-
  parity-or-leading on most public benchmarks as of early 2026. Watch.

6.5 FOUNDER GOVERNANCE

  Class B supervoting shares give Page + Brin >50% voting control with
  ~14% economic ownership. Historically benign but a structural
  governance flag.

6.6 MACRO / AD CYCLE

  ~73% of revenue is advertising. A severe 2027-2028 ad recession could
  cut Services growth by 5-10pts for 2-4 quarters. Cloud growth would
  partially insulate but not fully offset.

  Less acute than for pure-play ad-tech (META, TTD) because Search has
  long demonstrated counter-cyclical defensive characteristics (lower-
  funnel, performance-led).

6.7 WAYMO / OTHER BETS LOSS DRAG

  Other Bets operating loss widened from $4.4B (FY24) to $7.5B (FY25).
  Q4'25 included $2.1B Waymo employee comp charge. Feb 2026 $16B Waymo
  funding round (majority Alphabet-funded) signals continued investment.

  Optionality value (Waymo as ride-hailing TAM optionality + Verily as
  health TAM optionality) is meaningful but not in our base case
  valuation.
