0. WHY PUBM, WHY NOW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PubMatic is the portfolio's highest-conviction candidate for the empty small-cap
slot (currently 0% of the portfolio vs. a ~33% long-run target). It is the
sell-side mirror image of The Trade Desk (TTD), a name we already cover deeply:
where TTD is the leading independent DSP (the buy side), PUBM is a leading
independent SSP (the sell side). Owning both would let us construct a paired,
independent open-internet ad-tech position that does not depend on either Google
or Amazon's walled gardens.

The setup is a classic small-cap value-with-optionality shape:
  - The stock is near multi-year lows ($11 vs. a $76 era for MNDY-style
    drawdowns; 52-wk range $6.15-$13.88) on a revenue line that has gone
    BACKWARDS the last two years (-3% FY2025) due to a specific, identifiable,
    and self-limiting "legacy DSP" headwind plus lost 2024 political spend.
  - Underneath that optical decline, the UNDERLYING business (ex-legacy DSP)
    grew +13% in Q1 2026, with CTV +18% (ex-DSP), mobile app +25%, and emerging
    revenues +80%. Management guides to a return to reported growth in Q3 and
    double-digit growth in H2 2026 as the legacy DSP laps.
  - The balance sheet is fortress-grade for a company this size: $145M cash,
    ZERO debt, against a ~$517M market cap. Net cash is ~28% of the entire
    market cap.
  - 40 consecutive quarters of positive adjusted EBITDA and consistent FCF
    ($46M FY2025, $232M cumulative since 2021).
  - Two free options sit on top: (a) a credible AI/agentic ad story (AgenticOS)
    that is already ~14% of revenue and growing 80%+, and (b) the Google ad-tech
    antitrust remedy, where management frames each 1% of share gained as
    $50-75M of ~80%-margin revenue against a current ~4% share.

This is exactly the kind of misunderstood-incumbent-at-a-cheap-price that fits
our mandate. But "cheap and improving" is a thesis to be tested, not bought on
sight. This initiation establishes the facts; the deep dive and DCF must
confirm the asymmetry before any capital is committed.

1. BUSINESS DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PubMatic (founded 2006, Delaware; IPO'd on Nasdaq Dec 9, 2020 at symbol PUBM) is
an independent, AI-powered, sell-side advertising technology company. Its core
function: an independent Supply-Side Platform (SSP) that lets digital publishers
(websites, apps, streamers/CTV) monetize their ad inventory by connecting them,
in real time, to buyers (DSPs, agencies, advertisers).

Mechanics and economics:
  - PubMatic charges publishers a fee that is generally a PERCENTAGE of the
    value of the ad impressions monetized on its platform (a take rate on
    spend). It earns money only when it successfully monetizes an impression —
    its incentives are aligned with publisher yield and buyer ROI.
  - Revenue is reported on a NET basis: gross billings to buyers, less amounts
    paid to publishers, less SPO rebates. This is why accounts receivable
    ($337M) and accounts payable ($343M) dwarf reported revenue ($283M) — PUBM
    collects gross from buyers and pays publishers, keeping only its take.
  - Critically, PubMatic OWNS AND OPERATES its own hardware/software
    infrastructure globally rather than renting public cloud. At trillion-
    impressions-per-day scale, this is a structural cost advantage: cost per
    million impressions fell ~20% YoY even as volume grew ~26%. It also
    underpins the AI story (owned NVIDIA GPU + Triton inference).

Scale markers (FY2025 / Q1 2026):
  - ~1,980 premium publishers worldwide; ~28 of the top 30 global streamers.
  - >1 trillion impressions processed per day (94.2T in Q1 2026, +26% YoY).
  - SPO (Supply Path Optimization) = ~55-56% of total platform activity.
  - 1,030 employees: 322 US, 573 India (heavy, low-cost engineering base), 135
    elsewhere.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. PRODUCTS & SEGMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PubMatic does not report GAAP segments, but the business decomposes into a core
SSP plus a fast-growing "emerging revenue" layer and an AI layer on top.

Core SSP / monetization:
  - OpenWrap: omnichannel header-bidding solution (open-source Prebid based);
    now integrated with all 3 leading mobile mediation platforms (AppLovin MAX,
    Google AdMob, Unity LevelPlay) = >90% of global SDK inventory.
  - Channel mix (Q1 2026 YoY): CTV (Americas +13%, global ex-DSP +18%), mobile
    app (+25%+), display (+5%). CTV + mobile app + emerging together grew >20%
    and are now the majority of revenue.

Emerging revenue (Activate, Commerce Media/Convert, Connect, AI Solutions):
  - +80%+ YoY, ~14% of total revenue in Q1 2026.
  - Activate: PubMatic's direct-buying platform (buyers transact directly
    against premium supply in PubMatic's environment). Grew >3x YoY. When a
    buyer uses Activate, the WHOLE dollar stays in PubMatic's ecosystem
    (incremental SSP rev share on top of Activate rev share) — a structurally
    higher-value transaction than a third-party-DSP flow.
  - Connect: data platform aggregating 300+ data/commerce-media partners;
    enables sell-side audience targeting. Now augmented by Walmart Connect
    Select (first-party shopper data) and PayPal Ads ID (25B+ transactions,
    400M verified PayPal/Venmo accounts, deterministic identity).
  - Commerce media TAM cited at ~$18B.

AI / AgenticOS (launched Jan 2026 at CES):
  - 20+ operational AI agents for buyers and publishers; 1,000+ AI-powered
    deals transacted; 30+ live fully-autonomous end-to-end campaigns globally.
  - 80-90% campaign setup time savings; 30-40% CPM improvement and 40% more
    working media when used with Activate. Management frames agentic advertising
    as the "third transformation" (after RTB and mobile) and larger than both.
  - Pricing: Activate rev-share based today; subscription models being explored
    for some AI tools. AI is positioned as both a revenue driver AND an internal
    margin lever (engineering/finance/legal/customer-success productivity gains
    funding GTM reinvestment; headcount down YoY).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. CUSTOMERS & GO-TO-MARKET
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Four customer types: (1) publishers, (2) buyers (DSPs, agency holdcos,
independent agencies, advertisers), (3) data partners/curators, (4) retail/
commerce media participants.

Customer concentration — the central structural risk:
  - PubMatic depends on a LIMITED number of large DSPs for a large share of
    impressions purchased. Its two largest DSP relationships are Google and The
    Trade Desk. Both contracts auto-renew and are terminable for convenience on
    30 days' notice.
  - This concentration is the proximate cause of the last two years of pain: a
    "legacy DSP" buyer implemented bidding-methodology/platform changes (H2 2024
    and another in H2 2025) that materially reduced spend routed through PUBM.
    This is the single most important number to track — the company is
    diversifying away from it (mid-market DSP spend +20%, 50+ new DSPs added).

GTM:
  - Dual sales motion: publisher acquisition/relationship teams + buyer-side
    business development. SPO and Activate agreements signed directly with
    advertisers/agencies to pull spend onto the platform without proportional
    cost increases.
  - Geographic diversification working: APAC +25%, EMEA +10% in Q1 2026,
    offsetting an Americas -12% (legacy DSP drag).
  - Net dollar-based retention: 96% FY2025 (down from 107% FY2024), "flattish"
    in Q1 2026 — depressed by the legacy DSP. Management expects a return to
    >100% in H2 2026. NDR is the cleanest single proxy for thesis confirmation.

4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE & MOAT ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The SSP market is competitive and consolidating. Competitors:
  - Google's ad exchange (AdX / Google Ad Manager) — the ~60% gorilla, and the
    subject of the DOJ ad-tech antitrust case (the upside catalyst).
  - Magnite (the other scaled independent SSP, especially CTV).
  - Index Exchange, OpenX, Sharethrough, and smaller/regional private SSPs.
  - Amazon's Transparent Ad Marketplace and various walled-garden supply.

PubMatic's differentiation:
  - Independence/neutrality: it owns no media and does not arbitrage inventory,
    so its algorithmic decisions are unconflicted — a genuine trust advantage
    vs. Google, which sits on both sides of the auction.
  - Owned infrastructure: structural unit-cost advantage and a faster AI
    feedback loop (own GPUs, own data, end-to-end agentic execution).
  - Five claimed advantages (mgmt framing): (1) scale of supply (~2,000
    publishers / 100,000+ properties / 28 of top 30 streamers), (2) Activate
    direct-buy, (3) AgenticOS + 20 agents, (4) owned NVIDIA GPU infra, (5)
    Connect data platform (300+ partners). The claim is no competitor has all
    five.

MOAT VERDICT — NARROW, MODERATE confidence (preliminary):
  - In favor: real switching costs for publishers (deep integrations,
    header-bidding wrappers, customer-success embedding); scale economics in
    owned infra that improve with volume; a genuine independence/neutrality
    positioning the walled gardens structurally cannot replicate; an AI/data
    flywheel that compounds with each transaction.
  - Against: the SSP layer has historically been more commoditized and
    take-rate-pressured than the DSP layer; PubMatic does NOT control demand
    (its largest buyers can and did reroute spend with 30 days' notice); the
    open-internet SSP is squeezed between Google's owned exchange and the walled
    gardens. Take-rate compression is the structural long-term threat.
  - Net: a narrow moat, not a wide one. The investability case rests less on an
    impregnable moat and more on (a) a cheap price, (b) a fortress balance
    sheet, (c) a real (if narrow) independence + owned-infra edge, and (d) two
    free call options (AI + Google remedy).

6. KEY RISKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ranked by my preliminary weighting:

  (1) DSP CONCENTRATION / TAKE-RATE COMPRESSION (highest weight, ~30%): a small
      number of large DSPs (Google, TTD) drive a large share of impressions and
      can reroute spend on 30 days' notice. The legacy-DSP headwind is the live
      proof. Long-term SSP take-rate compression is the structural overhang.
  (2) AI DISRUPTION — DOUBLE-EDGED (~20%): AgenticOS is the bull case, but if
      buyers/LLMs (OpenAI's stated $100B ad ambition) disintermediate the SSP
      layer or compress take rates, PUBM is on the wrong side. Mgmt argues it
      partners; the deep dive must test the disintermediation tail.
  (3) RETURN-TO-GROWTH EXECUTION (~15%): the entire thesis hinges on H2 2026
      reacceleration to double digits. If Q3/Q4 do NOT reaccelerate as the
      legacy DSP laps, the "trough" reframes as "structural decline."
  (4) LEADERSHIP TURNOVER (~10%): two senior revenue-org exits mid-trough.
  (5) GOOGLE LITIGATION + ANTITRUST (~10%, net positive but uncertain): PUBM is
      in active litigation with Google AND a beneficiary of the DOJ remedy. The
      remedy is upside optionality; the litigation is a contained cost/risk.
  (6) PRIVACY / COOKIE / IDENTIFIER DEPRECATION + REGULATION (~10%): GDPR, CCPA,
      state laws, cookie/ID deprecation could impair targeting/data value.
  (7) MACRO / FX / SEASONALITY (~5%): ad spend is cyclical; Q1 seasonally weak;
      EUR/GBP FX a recurring headwind; revenue concentrated in Q4.

9. PRELIMINARY RATING & PIPELINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PRELIMINARY RATING: WATCH / ACCUMULATE ON CONFIRMATION.

PUBM is the right candidate for the empty small-cap slot and has the right
shape (cheap, net-cash, FCF-positive, narrow-moat, founder-led, with two free
options). But the thesis is explicitly contingent on the H2 2026 reacceleration
being real, and the deep dive must resolve several open questions before capital
is committed.

THESIS-MUST-HOLD conditions (to confirm in Deep Dive / subsequent prints):
  (i)   H2 2026 returns to reported growth (Q3) and double-digit growth (H2).
  (ii)  NDR returns toward / above 100% as the legacy DSP laps in mid-Q3.
  (iii) Adj. EBITDA margin re-expands toward the high-20s%/30% on the fixed-cost
        base as revenue scales (Q2 guide of $8-10M EBITDA on $68-70M = ~13% is
        the first checkpoint).
  (iv)  Emerging/AI revenue (AgenticOS) sustains 50%+ growth and shows a
        credible path to a monetization model (rev-share + subscription).
  (v)   A permanent global CRO is hired and the revenue org stabilizes.

THESIS-BREAK triggers (preliminary; 3+ = avoid/exit):
  - Q3 2026 does NOT return to reported YoY growth despite lapping the DSP.
  - NDR stuck below ~98% into Q4 2026.
  - Take-rate / SSP economics visibly compressing (gross margin breaking below
    ~60% structurally).
  - Emerging-revenue growth decelerates sharply (sub-30%), undercutting the AI
    optionality.
  - Another major DSP reroutes spend, or Google/TTD relationship deteriorates.

DEEP DIVE MARCHING ORDERS (Action 3):
  1. Decompose the "legacy DSP" headwind precisely: size it, confirm the
     mid-Q3 2026 lap timing, and stress-test whether the remaining DSP
     concentration is improving or merely shifting.
  2. Unit economics: take-rate trajectory, gross-margin durability, SBC-adjusted
     FCF margin, and the operating leverage of the owned-infra fixed-cost base.
  3. TAM + AgenticOS: size the agentic-advertising and commerce-media
     opportunity; assess the AI disintermediation tail (LLM/OpenAI ad ambition)
     vs. PUBM's partner positioning.
  4. Google ad-tech remedy scenario tree: probability-weight the share-gain
     optionality (each 1% ~ $50-75M at ~80% margin) WITHOUT putting it in the
     base case.
  5. Competitive dynamics vs. Magnite and Google over 5-10 years; SSP
     consolidation thesis.
  6. Management/governance: weigh the dual revenue-leader departures and the
     dual-class structure; capital-allocation track record.
  7. 5/10-year bull/base/bear scenarios and a thesis-break checklist.
  8. Explicit DCF marching orders: SBC-adjusted FCF underwriting, take-rate
     sensitivity, H2-reacceleration base case, net-cash floor, 10% and 12%
     hurdle rates, perpetuity-growth + exit-multiple terminal values.

No position. No trade. This initiation advances PUBM from candidate (#14, 6/10
on the 2026-05-06 scan; #1 small-cap initiation priority) into the active
research pipeline. Deep Dive (Action 3) is the next eligible action.
