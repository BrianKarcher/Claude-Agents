1. BUSINESS DESCRIPTION & HISTORY
================================================================================

The Trade Desk, Inc. (TTD) is the largest independent demand-side platform (DSP)
in the world, headquartered in Ventura, California. Founded in 2009 by Jeff Green
and Dave Pickles — both veterans of Microsoft's AdECN acquisition — the company
enables advertisers and agencies to plan, execute, and optimize data-driven
programmatic advertising campaigns across a unified, cloud-based platform.

TTD went public on the Nasdaq in September 2016. From its IPO through FY2025,
revenue compounded at approximately 31% annually (2020-2024 CAGR), growing from
~$202M at IPO to $2.9B in FY2025. The company joined the Nasdaq-100 in July 2023.
Customer retention has remained above 95% for multiple consecutive years.

A defining strategic choice is TTD's strict "buy-side only" mandate: the company
does not own or represent ad inventory, ensuring its interests are fully aligned
with advertiser clients seeking the best return on media spend. This stands in
contrast to Google's vertically integrated stack, which operates on both the buy
side and sell side.

================================================================================
2. PRODUCTS & SEGMENTS
================================================================================

TTD is a single-segment business from a financial reporting standpoint. In
December 2025, the company reorganized internally into dedicated business units
for CTV, Retail Media, and Identity to sharpen go-to-market focus.

CORE PLATFORM
-------------
Self-service DSP: A cloud-based interface where traders at agencies and brands
build, target, bid on, and measure programmatic ad campaigns in real time across
display, video, audio, native, and connected TV (CTV) inventory.

Kokai: The AI-powered platform overhaul launched in late 2024 and fully rolled
out through 2025. Kokai processes over 10 million ad requests per second using
deep learning and includes "Koa Adaptive Trading Modes," which automate bidding
based on real-time ROI signals. As of early 2026, over 85% of clients use Kokai;
adopters report up to 20% improvements in cost-per-acquisition (CPA).

IDENTITY INFRASTRUCTURE
------------------------
Unified ID 2.0 (UID2): An open-source identity solution developed by TTD to
replace third-party cookies with encrypted, hashed email identifiers. Works
across web, mobile, and CTV. Adopted by Roku, Dish/Sling, LG smart TVs, and
major publishers. UID2-based display ads deliver a 2.9x higher click-through
rate and 2.4x better CPA vs. non-UID2 ads per TTD case studies.

CONNECTED TV (CTV) & VENTURA OS
---------------------------------
CTV accounts for approximately 50% of TTD's total revenue mix as of Q4 2025.
TTD accesses CTV inventory across Disney+, Netflix, Warner Bros. Discovery,
Peacock, Paramount+, Hulu, and others.

Ventura OS: A proprietary CTV operating system announced in late 2024, designed
to challenge Roku and Amazon Fire TV. In February 2026, TTD launched the "Ventura
Ecosystem," partnering with VIDAA (on 50M+ Hisense/Toshiba devices) and Nexxen,
pivoting toward an ecosystem collaboration rather than a standalone OS buildout.
DirecTV announced plans to develop a custom Ventura UI.

RETAIL MEDIA
-------------
TTD has integrated with major retailers including Walmart and Kroger, enabling
advertisers to target shoppers via anonymized first-party purchase data and close
the measurement loop between ad exposure and in-store or online conversion.

DATA & MEASUREMENT
-------------------
Third-party data marketplace: Advertisers activate audience segments from
hundreds of data providers within the platform.
Enterprise APIs: Programmatic access for agency trading desks and large
enterprise advertisers.

================================================================================
3. CUSTOMERS & GO-TO-MARKET
================================================================================

TTD sells primarily to advertising agencies and agency trading desks (ATDs) —
the media buying arms of holding companies such as WPP, Omnicom, Publicis, IPG,
and Dentsu — as well as directly to large brands. Customer retention above 95%
reflects deep platform stickiness and high switching costs.

The go-to-market motion is direct sales through a global salesforce, supplemented
by self-service access for sophisticated agency traders. TTD charges a platform
fee as a percentage of managed ad spend — typically 10-20% on smaller budgets,
negotiated lower for the largest clients.

Notable tension: Publicis distributed an internal memo in late 2025/early 2026
advising select clients to reduce transactions on TTD, citing auditing firm
FirmDecisions findings that alleged TTD improperly applied its DSP fee to certain
other charges. TTD disputed these characterizations. The episode highlights
structural tension between TTD and holding companies.

================================================================================
4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE & MOAT ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

MARKET STRUCTURE
-----------------
The global DSP market is dominated by two players: Google's DV360 (~40% share)
and The Trade Desk (~20%). Amazon DSP is a rapidly rising third force,
particularly after securing Netflix as its primary programmatic partner in 2025.
The remaining market fragments among Microsoft Xandr, MediaMath, Basis, and
agency proprietary stacks.

MOAT SOURCES
-------------
Independence / Trust: TTD's buy-side-only model creates a structural trust
advantage over Google and Amazon. This independence is a compounding asset as
antitrust scrutiny on Google intensifies.

Data network effects: More advertisers on the platform generate more bidding
signal data for the Koa AI engine, improving performance outcomes, which attracts
more advertisers in a reinforcing loop. TTD processes trillions of bid-stream
events annually.

Switching costs: Advertisers invest heavily in training traders, integrating
audience segments, and building reporting dashboards on TTD. The >95% retention
rate confirms switching costs are substantial.

UID2 as infrastructure layer: If UID2 becomes the de facto post-cookie identity
standard, TTD sits at the center of the identity graph for much of the open
internet.

Open internet scale leadership: TTD is the largest pure-play buyer of open-
internet inventory; premium publishers have strong incentive to prioritize TTD
integrations.

MOAT RISKS
-----------
- Amazon DSP's first-party data advantage (Prime purchase history from 200M+
  members) is structurally difficult to replicate.
- Google antitrust: If courts force a divestiture of AdX, the open internet
  becomes more competitive — a potential TTD tailwind, though outcome uncertain.
- Holding company tensions could accelerate proprietary in-house trading desk
  buildouts, reducing reliance on TTD.

Gartner rating: TTD 4.6 stars vs. Amazon DSP 4.4 stars.
Frost Radar 2025 named TTD a "Growth Leader" in DSPs.

5. MANAGEMENT QUALITY
================================================================================

JEFF GREEN — Founder, Chairman & CEO
--------------------------------------
Co-founded TTD in 2009 alongside Dave Pickles after both spent two years at
Microsoft following the 2007 acquisition of their prior company AdECN. Green
holds Class B supervoting shares (10 votes each), giving him effective control.

Green is a visionary operator who compounded TTD revenue at ~31% annually from
2020 to 2024, built customer retention above 95%, and guided TTD into the
Nasdaq-100. Strong equity ownership aligns him with shareholders.

CFO INSTABILITY — GOVERNANCE YELLOW FLAG
------------------------------------------
Laura Schenkein served as CFO from June 2023 until mid-2025. She was replaced by
Alex Kayyal (former Lightspeed Venture Partners GP), who was terminated after
just five months on January 24, 2026. Tahnil Davis, an 11-year TTD veteran and
Chief Accounting Officer, was named interim CFO. Two CFO transitions in
approximately 30 months is an elevated governance concern.

OVERALL ASSESSMENT
-------------------
Founder-led with exceptional long-term track record (POSITIVE).
CFO stability is a current concern requiring monitoring (WATCH).
Capital allocation has been sensible: net cash position, no aggressive dilution,
strong FCF generation alongside R&D investment (Kokai, Ventura, UID2).

================================================================================
6. KEY RISKS
================================================================================

1. Growth Deceleration: Revenue growth slowed from ~26% in FY2024 to 18% in
   FY2025, and Q1 2026 guidance of "at least $678M" implies only 10% YoY growth.
   Causes include lapping strong 2024 political ad spend, CTV market maturation,
   and macro pressure on CPG and automotive advertisers.

2. Amazon DSP Competition: Amazon has become TTD's most direct and best-resourced
   rival. Its first-party shopper data from 200M+ Prime members is a structural
   advantage TTD cannot replicate. Amazon secured Netflix as its primary
   programmatic partner in 2025.

3. Walled Garden Re-acceleration: Meta and Google have regained advertiser
   confidence as proven ROI engines during macroeconomic uncertainty. Budget
   flight to walled gardens directly pressures TTD's open-internet positioning.

4. CFO Instability: Two CFO changes in ~30 months raises questions about internal
   financial leadership.

5. Agency Holding Company Tensions: The Publicis situation highlights structural
   tension. If major holding companies accelerate proprietary trading desk
   buildouts, it could meaningfully impact revenue growth.

6. Ventura OS Execution Risk: The February 2026 pivot to an "ecosystem" approach
   suggests the standalone OS path is proving harder than initially communicated.

7. Dual-Class Share Structure: Jeff Green controls voting outcomes via Class B
   shares. Minority shareholders have limited recourse on governance decisions.

8. Macroeconomic Sensitivity: Digital advertising is cyclically sensitive. A U.S.
   recession or sustained tariff-driven inflation could deepen budget cuts in
   cyclical verticals (auto, CPG).

Investment Thesis
------------------
The Trade Desk occupies a structurally advantaged position at the intersection of
three durable secular growth vectors: the migration of TV ad budgets to streaming
(CTV), the post-cookie identity revolution (UID2), and AI-driven programmatic
buying by the world's largest advertisers. The company is the only scaled,
independent DSP not controlled by a walled garden — a trust asset that compounds
in value as Google faces antitrust consequences.

At approximately 12x trailing free cash flow, the market is pricing in near-
permanent growth impairment from the Q4 2025/Q1 2026 deceleration. We view this
as an overreaction to cyclical factors (election-year lapping, macro softness in
auto and CPG) and execution missteps (CFO instability) rather than structural
erosion of the moat.

Primary risk: Amazon's continued aggressive subsidization of its DSP and the
possibility that holding company tensions (Publicis) spread to other agency
groups, which would represent genuine share-of-wallet erosion.

THREE CATALYSTS NEEDED BEFORE FULL BUY:
  1. Permanent CFO hire
  2. Evidence of re-acceleration beyond the 10% Q1 2026 guidance floor
  3. Further Ventura Ecosystem traction
